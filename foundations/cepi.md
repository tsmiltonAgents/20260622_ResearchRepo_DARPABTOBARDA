# CEPI — Coalition for Epidemic Preparedness Innovations

## Introduction

CEPI is a global partnership — launched at the World Economic Forum in Davos in January 2017 — that finances and coordinates vaccine development against emerging infectious diseases with epidemic/pandemic potential. Headquartered in Oslo, Norway, led since April 2017 by CEO Dr. Richard Hatchett. Conceived as a response to the world's failure to have vaccines ready for the 2014–16 West African Ebola outbreak; founding rationale traces to a July 2015 *NEJM* paper co-authored by Jeremy Farrar (Wellcome), Stanley Plotkin, and Adel Mahmoud.

CEPI is a non-profit funder, not a developer: it issues calls for proposals, awards grants/contracts to vaccine developers, and embeds equitable-access provisions. Figures below are largely "up to"/ceiling amounts; uncertain figures are flagged.

## Section 1 — Programmes and Initiatives

### 1.1 Founding (2017, Davos)
Founders: Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, Wellcome Trust, Government of Norway, Government of India, World Economic Forum. Initial funding **~US$460M** in five-year pledges (Japan $125M, Norway $120M, Gates $100M, Wellcome $100M, Germany). By Feb 2020 ~$760M raised; UK pledged £210M (Mar 2020).

### 1.2 Priority Pathogens (WHO R&D Blueprint)
MERS-CoV, Lassa, Nipah, Rift Valley fever, Chikungunya, SARS-CoV-2 (added 2020), and **Disease X**. Oct 2018 estimate: ~US$2.8–3.7B to develop one vaccine per Blueprint disease.

### 1.3 COVAX Role
CEPI co-led **COVAX** (vaccines pillar of the ACT-Accelerator) with Gavi, WHO, UNICEF (2020–2023), leading the R&D portfolio and securing dose access. COVAX delivered ~2 billion doses to 146 countries.

### 1.4 100 Days Mission & "$3.5B" Plan
Goal: a vaccine against a new pandemic threat **within 100 days** of recognition. CEPI's **5-year ~US$3.5B plan ("CEPI 2.0", ~2022–2026)**: compress timelines, develop a broadly protective Betacoronavirus vaccine, build a prototype "vaccine library." March 2022 London summit pledged ~US$1.5–1.6B. Successor "CEPI 3.0" (2027–2031) published.

### 1.5 Vaccine Library / Disease X / Rapid-Response Platforms
Global Vaccine Library; rapid-response platforms (mRNA, saRNA, circular RNA, ChAdOx). Early platform investments: Imperial College London (saRNA), University of Queensland (molecular clamp), CureVac (RNA Printer). Disease X commitments incl. SK bioscience (mRNA library) and University of Oxford.

### 1.6 Partnerships
WHO (R&D Blueprint), Gavi (COVAX), EU/EC, EDCTP, FDA, EMA, UNICEF, MSF, PATH, IAVI; Ind-CEPI (with India).

### 1.7 Manufacturing Network
Reserved capacity for >1 billion doses during COVID; goal of 1–2 billion doses regional capacity activatable in days. Global South investments: Bio Farma Indonesia (up to $15M); BioNTech Kigali, Rwanda (up to $145M, May 2024). CMC rapid-response framework.

## Notes on uncertainty
Most awards are "up to"/maximum, milestone-based. Novavax reported as $384M and $399M (different dates). Clover ~$328M cumulative. Biological E and Bharat/Sydney/ExcellGene amounts undisclosed. The $25M Nipah subunit award is most reliably **Auro+PATH** (not Profectus). Affinivax's CEPI tie is the Dec 2021 COVID-variant award, not Lassa.
