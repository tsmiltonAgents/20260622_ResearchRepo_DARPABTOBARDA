# The Novo Nordisk Foundation (NNF)

## Introduction
Danish enterprise foundation; by assets the world's largest charitable foundation (owns Novo Holdings, which holds ~28%/~77% votes in Novo Nordisk A/S). AUM ~DKK 1,060B end-2024 → ~DKK 694B end-2025. Annual grants crossed DKK 10B (~€1.35B) in 2024. **Infectious disease is a small slice** (~1.3% of the 2024 grant base, ~DKK 110M) — but flagship initiatives are large, multi-year, and lumpy. All amounts are NNF's published conversions; uncertain items flagged.

---

## Section 1 — Programmes

### Pandemic preparedness
- **Global Pathogen Analysis Platform (GPAP)** — up to **DKK 200M / 6 yrs** (Oct 2025); AI genomic-pathogen platform; hosted at DTU, with U Copenhagen, Statens Serum Institut, Imperial College London.

### Antiviral discovery
- **Pandemic Antiviral Discovery (PAD)** — launched Mar 2022, **up to USD 90M collective** (NNF + **Open Philanthropy** + **Gates Foundation**); phase-2-ready oral antivirals for future pandemics. **$20M to 11 projects** for pandemic influenza (May 2024). Individual funder shares not disclosed.
- **DNDi/THSTI** broad-spectrum antiviral renewal — €2,395,902 (2026–2028).

### Vaccines & biomanufacturing
- **NNF Initiative for Vaccines and Immunity (NIVI)** — **up to DKK 1.8B (USD 260M)** (Dec 2023) for vaccines vs TB, influenza, Group A Strep via mucosal immunity. Research arm at U Copenhagen (up to DKK 1.425B); development/manufacturing arm = **NNF Vaccine Accelerator (NVAC)**.
- **AIM Bio** — DKK 182.7M (USD 27M, 2019) biologics-manufacturing science (NC State + DTU).
- Novo Nordisk Prize (DKK 5M, 2022) to Karikó/Weissman/Şahin/Türeci for COVID mRNA vaccines (recognition, not R&D).

### Antimicrobial resistance (AMR)
- **REPAIR Impact Fund** — DKK 1B / USD 165M (2018, via Novo Holdings); ~12 companies; now closed to new proposals.
- **AMR Action Fund** — NNF DKK 330M (USD 50M) into the ~$1B global fund (2020).
- **CARB-X** — up to USD 25M over 3 yrs (Jan 2024).
- **Three-funder partnership (Gates + NNF + Wellcome)** — USD 300M, **each $100M** (May 2024), AMR first; **Gr-ADI (Gram-Negative Antibiotic Discovery Innovator)** USD 50M launch (Feb 2025) → **USD 60M to 18 projects in 17 countries** (Jan 2026, managed by RTI International). Per-funder Gr-ADI split undisclosed.
- **WHO framework agreement** — up to DKK 380M (~USD 57M, 2025–2028; AMR one of four themes).
- **PIG-PARADIGM** (DKK 150M, 2022, One Health/pig sector); **Infectious Diseases Catalyst Grants** (recurring; DKK 60M for the 2026 call; surveillance excluded).

### CEPI / 100 Days Mission — negative finding
**No evidence of a direct NNF financial contribution to CEPI.** NNF's alignment with the 100 Days Mission is thematic (via PAD/NIVI/GPAP), not a CEPI grant.

### COVID-19 response (2020–2021, ~DKK 364M total)
Testcenter Danmark (up to DKK 250M to Statens Serum Institut); domestic emergency grants (DKK 82.5M/45 projects); emergency ethanol (DKK 17.5M); international initiatives (DKK 14.4M/15).

## Section 2 — Grantees / Awardees (representative)

- **DTU (Technical University of Denmark)** — DTU Biosustain (DKK 700M est. 2011; DKK 750M 2021–25; >DKK 2B cumulative); wastewater AMR surveillance (DKK 60M, 2017); GPAP (DKK 200M, 2025); Microbiome Health Initiative Phase 1 (DKK 150M/USD 22M, 2024).
- **University of Copenhagen** — NIVI Research Center (up to DKK 1.425B, 2023); FluZooMark (DKK 59.9M, 2019, zoonotic/pandemic flu).
- **Aarhus University** — PIG-PARADIGM lead; Catalyst grants (*Aspergillus*, interferon-λ); Distinguished Investigator antiviral defence (DKK 10M, 2018).
- **University of Southern Denmark** — Catalyst grants (polyene-resistant Candida, pleuromutilins).
- **International:** REPAIR investments (Mutabilis €7M, IBT Vaccines $3.9M, etc.); AMR Action Fund; CARB-X; Gr-ADI consortium (18 teams/17 countries, incl. Imperial); DNDi+THSTI (€2.4M); NC State (AIM Bio).

## Key flags
Gr-ADI per-funder split undisclosed; NIVI DKK 1.425B research-arm figure from U Copenhagen pages; **no direct NNF→CEPI contribution** (thematic alignment only); no verified NNF→GARDP grant; DKK 392M AMR cumulative is a 2020 snapshot.
