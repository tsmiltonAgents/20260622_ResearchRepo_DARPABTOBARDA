# Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation

## Introduction
Since 2000, the single most influential philanthropic actor in global infectious-disease control — co-founding the institutions that finance vaccine access and epidemic-preparedness R&D (Gavi, CEPI) and seeding COVID-19 treatment/testing partnerships. End-2025: endowment ~$89B, 2025 charitable support ~$8.5B, cumulative giving ~$110.9B. May 2025: announced a **spend-down of ~$200B over ~20 years, closing 31 Dec 2045** (estimate). Grantees below are **major/representative** with verifiable figures; many big dollars flow through intermediaries (Gavi, CEPI, PATH/MVI) rather than named end-recipients. The committed-grants database resisted automated fetching; figures derive from press releases, recipient disclosures, and peer-reviewed literature.

## 1. Programmes and Initiatives

### Global Health Division
Predates the 2000 merger (Gordon Perkin named first global-health ED Sept 1999; William Foege advisor). Develops high-impact interventions against leading causes of death in developing countries. Currently led by Dr. Trevor Mundel (since 2011). *No verified standalone cumulative dollar total exists.*

### Vaccine Development & Delivery
**>US$30.6B committed to vaccines since 2000** (verified June 2025) — discovery, development, distribution.

### Grand Challenges family
GCGH launched **Jan 2003** ($200M to FNIH; +$250M May 2005 = **$450M**); GCE launched **2008** ($100M, $100k grants); cumulative Grand Challenges (all partners): **$1.6B across 3,800+ grants in 118 countries** (2003–2023).

### Gavi, the Vaccine Alliance
Largest grantee. Founding pledge **$750M** (1999/2000); subsequent pledges incl. $1.6B (2021–2025) and **$1.6B "Gavi 6.0" (2026–2030)**. **Cumulative ~$7.7B since 2000** (current; supersedes stale $4.1B/$6.1B figures on Gavi pages).

### CEPI
**Co-founder** (Jan 2017); initial **$100M**; **$150M** in Jan 2022 (half of a $300M joint pledge with Wellcome); $20M COVID (2020).

### Institute for Disease Modeling (IDM)
Founded 2008 (Global Good/Intellectual Ventures); **transitioned into the Global Health Division mid-2020** (EMOD, Covasim). No verified funding/headcount figures.

### Pandemic-preparedness advocacy
Bill Gates's 2015 TED talk and 2022 book *How to Prevent the Next Pandemic* (proposed **GERM team**, ~3,000 staff, ~$1B/yr — a proposal, never established). Founding philanthropic donor (with Wellcome) to the World Bank **Pandemic Fund** (2022). *The circulating "$15M Gates" Pandemic Fund figure is unverified and appears to be Rockefeller's.* **Total COVID-19 response >US$2B since Jan 2020** (mix of grants, at-risk financing, redirected funds).

### COVID-19 Therapeutics Accelerator (CTA)
Launched **10 Mar 2020** with Wellcome + Mastercard; **up to $125M** seed (uneven: Gates $50M, Wellcome $50M, Mastercard $25M). ~$98.6M awarded by Oct 2020. Later donors: CZI ($25M), UK FCDO (up to £40M).

### Medicines Patent Pool (MPP)
**No verified direct Gates→MPP grant** — MPP's funders list does not include Gates; MPP was established by Unitaid (2010), and Gates is a Unitaid donor (indirect). On COVID, Gates funded generic makers directly (e.g., **up to $120M, Oct 2021** for generic molnupiravir).

### mRNA / Manufacturing-in-Africa
**Quantoom mRNA package — $40M (Oct 9, 2023)**: $20M Quantoom (Belgium), $5M Institut Pasteur de Dakar, $5M Biovac (South Africa), $10M reserved. **Aspen (South Africa) — $30M (Dec 2022)** ($15M Gates + $15M CEPI). *Gates is NOT a listed funder of the WHO mRNA hub at Afrigen* (routed money to manufacturers directly). AVMA (Gavi, Jun 2024, up to $1.2B/10yr): Gates a founding partner.

## 2. Grantees and Awardees (major/representative)

- **Gavi** — ~$7.7B cumulative (largest grantee).
- **CEPI** — $100M (2017), $20M (2020), $150M (2022).
- **WHO** — ~$4.0B 2010–2023 (~9.5% of WHO revenue, 2nd-largest funder); ~$5.5B cumulative 2000–2024 (~59% polio); ~$761M 2024–25 (became #1 voluntary contributor after US withdrawal). *(Different bases — don't sum.)*
- **PATH** — $168.7M (2008, Malaria Vaccine Initiative); $107.6M (2005, RTS,S); $50M (1999 founding MVI).
- **IAVI** — $100M (2001 challenge grant); $25M (1999).
- **IVI (Seoul)** — $40M (2000, DOMI cholera/typhoid/shigellosis); $13.4M (2009 cholera); ~$15.7M (~2019 typhoid Vi-DT).
- **Institut Pasteur** — project-specific (~$1.15M 2024 Shigella); larger to Institut Pasteur de Dakar ($5M in 2023 mRNA package).
- **University of Washington / IHME** — $105M (2007 founding); $279M/10yr (2017); ~$210M (~2016 building).
- **Imperial College London** — Target Malaria gene-drive ($35M 2016, ~$75M cumulative); $14.5M (2018).
- **Johns Hopkins** — $44.7M (2004, CREATE TB consortium).
- **University of Oxford** — R21/Matrix-M malaria, ChAdOx1/AstraZeneca COVID (limited Gates-specific dollar disclosure; ~£1.6M to pre-pandemic ChAdOx platform).
- **CTA grantees** — University of Washington, MORU/Oxford, La Jolla Institute ($20M initial Mar 2020); TOGETHER trial (fluvoxamine), COVID Moonshot/DNDi.
- **mRNA hub partners** — Afrigen (~$0 confirmed direct Gates funding); Biovac (~$5.24M, Nov 2023).
- **Diagnostics** — FIND (up to $30M 2003 founding; $62M 2007 TB; $3.88M 2021); COVID rapid testing (up to $100M Sept 2020 volume guarantees to Abbott/SD Biosensor at ≤$5/test); Africa Pathogen Genomics Initiative (~$45M Gates share of $100M, 2020).

## Key figures at a glance
Vaccines $30.6B (since 2000); Gavi $7.7B; GCGH $450M; GCE $100M; CEPI $100M/$150M; CTA seed $125M (Gates $50M); COVID response >$2B; molnupiravir up to $120M; mRNA/Quantoom $40M; WHO ~$5.5B cumulative; PATH/MVI $168.7M; UW/IHME $279M/10yr; FIND $62M; endowment $89B.

## Material corrections / flags
Gavi $7.7B supersedes stale figures; no standalone Global Health Division total; no verified direct Gates→MPP grant (indirect via Unitaid; funded generic makers directly); CTA split uneven; Afrigen reportedly unfunded by Gates directly; "$15M Gates" Pandemic Fund is likely Rockefeller's; WHO figures measured differently (don't sum); IDM/GERM/$55M-mRNA/IVI-typhoid-year uncertain; $200B spend-down is an estimate.
