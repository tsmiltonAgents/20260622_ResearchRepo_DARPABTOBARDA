# Open Philanthropy (Coefficient Giving)

## Introduction
Open Philanthropy (**rebranded "Coefficient Giving" in November 2025**) is one of the largest philanthropic funders of biosecurity and pandemic preparedness — a focus area it began funding ~2014–2015, ~5 years before COVID-19. Founded by Facebook co-founder **Dustin Moskovitz** and **Cari Tuna**; primary funder is their foundation **Good Ventures**. Figures from Open Philanthropy's grants database and grantee/press announcements; representative not exhaustive; legacy openphilanthropy.org grant pages now redirect to coefficientgiving.org (blocks fetchers), so some figures are corroborated via Good Ventures mirror / LongtermWiki.

---

## Section 1 — Programmes
Biosecurity and Pandemic Preparedness (BPP) sits within the **Global Catastrophic Risks** portfolio; central concern is global catastrophic biological risks, emphasizing technological/pathogen-agnostic defenses. Approach: proactive hits-based grantmaking + open RFPs (incl. an RFI on germicidal far-UVC), regranting vehicles (e.g. Good Forever ~$10M pool), and fellowships. An independent tracker recorded ~**$90M across ~50 grants to ~33 orgs, 2015–2021** (partial/dated; total now higher). Key BPP staff: Andrew Snyder-Beattie (Director), Chris Bakerlee (Program Officer, far-UVC).

**Themes funded:** far-UVC / germicidal UV air disinfection; metagenomic sequencing / early detection; nucleic-acid synthesis screening; next-generation PPE / respirators; pandemic-preparedness policy & governance; biosecurity research & diagnostics.
