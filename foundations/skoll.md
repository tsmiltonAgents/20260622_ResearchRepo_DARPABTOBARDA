# Skoll Foundation, Skoll Global Threats Fund & Ending Pandemics

## Introduction
Three related entities founded/backed by Jeff Skoll (eBay's first president): the **Skoll Foundation** (1999, social-entrepreneurship grantmaker — mostly adjacent to pandemic surveillance); the **Skoll Global Threats Fund (SGTF)** (2009–2017, ~$100M endowment, whose pandemics program built much of the participatory/digital disease-surveillance work below); and **Ending Pandemics** (independent from Jan 1, 2018, led by Dr. Mark Smolinski). Pandemic preparedness lived primarily in **SGTF → Ending Pandemics**, not the Skoll Foundation. **Grant-by-grant amounts are largely not disclosed**; most figures rest on secondary aggregators (flagged).

---

## Section 1 — Programmes

### Skoll Global Threats Fund (2009–2017)
Founded 2009 by Jeff Skoll with **Larry Brilliant** (ex-Google.org) as CEO; ~$100M endowment; five threat areas (pandemics, climate, water, nuclear, Middle East conflict). Pandemics program ("Ending Pandemics in Our Lifetime") created by **Mark Smolinski in 2012**. **Sunset end-2017**, spinning pandemics work into the independent nonprofit **Ending Pandemics**.

### Ending Pandemics (2018–)
Mission: find/verify/contain outbreaks faster everywhere. Five pillars: participatory surveillance, One Health, epidemic intelligence, regional collaboration (CORDS), timeliness metrics. Transitioning into the **Ending Pandemics Academy** at the University of Arizona (launched Nov 7, 2025; $5M gift from the Carlyle One Health Group; Smolinski holds the Jeff Skoll Endowed Chair).

### Key programs / surveillance approaches
- **Flu Near You (2011)** — participatory ILI symptom reporting (APHA + HealthMap/Boston Children's + SGTF); later → Outbreaks Near Me.
- **EpiHacks** — 5-day co-creation sprints (first: Phnom Penh, Aug 2013); ~12 EpiHacks 2013–2018, 500+ professionals, 29 countries.
- **PODD (Thailand)** — community-owned One Health digital surveillance (from EpiHack Chiang Mai 2014; Chiang Mai University + Opendream); later won the Trinity Challenge grand prize.
- **EpiCore** — outbreak-verification network (epidemiologists in 145+ countries; with HealthMap, ISID/ProMED, TEPHINET; operational 2016).
- **Mass-gathering surveillance (Brazil)** — Saúde na Copa (2014 World Cup), Guardiões da Saúde (2016 Rio).
- **Cambodia 115 Hotline** (2016); **AfyaData/DODRES** (EpiHack Tanzania 2014, SACIDS/Sokoine); **DoctorMe** (Thailand); **timeliness metrics & Mini Grants** (~17 countries).

### Skoll Foundation (broader)
Founded 1999; endowed the Skoll Centre for Social Entrepreneurship at Oxford (2003, $7.5M); runs the Skoll World Forum. **COVID-19 response:** new **$100M gift** (Apr 24, 2020), ~quadrupling 2020 grantmaking to ~$200M — priorities: testing/contact-tracing tools, respiratory devices/equipment for LMICs, Africa focus.

## Section 2 — Grantees / Awardees (most amounts NOT public)

### SGTF / Ending Pandemics surveillance grantees & partners
- **CORDS** (Connecting Organizations for Regional Disease Surveillance) — co-founded/supported by SGTF (with Rockefeller, NTI, Peterson Foundation, Fondation Mérieux); adopted as an Ending Pandemics program (2019). Member networks: MECIDS, MBDS, SACIDS, EAIDSNet, APEIR, SECID (~28 countries).
- **ProMED / ISID** (~$1.3M over 2010–2016, single aggregator); **HealthMap / Boston Children's** (Flu Near You; >$1.6M across six grants, aggregator); **TEPHINET**; **Task Force for Global Health** (>$2.9M, aggregator); **Chiang Mai University** (PODD, ~$2M 2014, secondary); **SACIDS/Sokoine** (AfyaData); **InSTEDD iLab SE Asia** (115 Hotline); **Epitrack/Brazilian MoH** (mass-gathering).

### Skoll Foundation — global-health Award recipients
Skoll Award (now ~$2M unrestricted/awardee): Riders for Health (2006), Partners In Health (2008), mothers2mothers (2008), VisionSpring (2009), Health Leads (2011), Medic Mobile (~2014), Living Goods (2016), Last Mile Health (2017), Community Health Impact Coalition (2025), Healthy Learners (2025).
