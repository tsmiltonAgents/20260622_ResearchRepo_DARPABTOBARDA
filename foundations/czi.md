# Chan Zuckerberg Initiative (CZI)

## Introduction
Founded 2015 by Priscilla Chan and Mark Zuckerberg; among the world's largest private biomedical funders. Science programme aims to "cure, prevent, or manage" disease; CZI committed **at least $10B to basic science over 2025–2035** (>2x the ~$4B of its first decade). Much pandemic-relevant work is conducted **internally via the Chan Zuckerberg Biohub Network** rather than as external grants, so external-grantee dollar figures are limited. Representative not exhaustive; CZI is broad (imaging, AI "virtual cell", neuroscience) — only infectious-disease/pandemic threads covered.

---

## Section 1 — Programmes

### Biohub model
**Chan Zuckerberg Biohub – San Francisco** (2016): CZI's first science investment, **$600M commitment**, independent collaboration of **UCSF, UC Berkeley, Stanford**; co-led by Stephen Quake and Joseph DeRisi. **+up to $1B (Dec 2021) to extend through 2031.**

### CZ ID / IDseq — metagenomic pathogen detection
Free, open-source, cloud-based platform screening metagenomic NGS data hypothesis-free to identify viruses/bacteria/fungi/parasites by genomic signature; positioned for **real-time global disease surveillance** (czid.org).

### COVID-19 sequencing & the CLIAHUB
CZ Biohub + CZI + UCSF stood up a CLIA testing lab ("CLIAHUB") in ~8 days; processed **>165,000 free COVID tests** over ~7 months. Partnered with California on the **California COVID Tracker** (sequenced >11,550 samples) — chronicled in Michael Lewis's *The Premonition*.

### Biohub Network expansion (2023)
**CZ Biohub Chicago** (U Chicago, Northwestern, UIUC; ~$250M CZI + $25M state; sensor tech for inflammation/disease); **CZ Biohub New York** (Columbia, Rockefeller, Yale; ~$300M incl. city/state; bioengineer immune cells to sense early disease).

### Single-cell biology / Human Cell Atlas
Foundational funder since 2016 (immune-system mapping relevant to vaccines/infection immunity); e.g. $28M for ancestrally diverse donor data (16 teams/31 countries); single-cell tech applied to COVID-19 progression.

### Pathogen genomics / global biosurveillance
Goal of a "global network of disease detectives" combining CZ ID, training, and partner labs for early warning.

## Section 2 — Grantees / Awardees (mostly internal to Biohub investigators)

- **Biohub SF Investigators** (UCSF/Berkeley/Stanford): 1st cohort 2017 — 47 investigators ~$1M each/5yr; 2nd cohort 2022 — 86 investigators ~$1M each. (UC Berkeley alone ~$14.5M.)
- **CZ ID partners in low-resource settings:** Dr. Senjuti Saha (Child Health Research Foundation, Dhaka) — traced a meningitis cluster to chikungunya; Cambodian fever study (464 patients, PNAS); ~10 Gates Foundation Grand Challenges grants piloted CZ ID.
- **COVID-19 testing/sequencing partners:** UCSF, State of California (CDPH/public-health labs), Stanford collaborators.
- **External grant programmes:** Human Cell Atlas pilot RFAs (e.g. NYU Abu Dhabi immune cell atlas; Oxford Kennedy Institute).

## Key flags
Per-grantee amounts for CZ ID partners and most HCA awards not consistently disclosed; "~$1M/investigator" figures are reported approximations; CZI's post-2025 AI/"virtual cell" pivot may reshape infectious-disease emphasis.
