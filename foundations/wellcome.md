# Wellcome Trust

## Introduction
Wellcome (the Wellcome Trust) is one of the world's largest charitable research foundations, funded from a self-sustaining endowment (~£39.9B portfolio in 2024/25; ~£1.9B charitable spend; a 10-year £16B commitment to 2032). A uniquely influential actor in global infectious-disease research and pandemic preparedness — often a **co-founder and anchor funder** of multilateral initiatives rather than only a grant-maker. Long history in tropical/infectious-disease research (overseas units since the 1970s–80s). In **2020–2021**, under then-director Sir Jeremy Farrar, Wellcome reorganised around three challenges — **Infectious Disease, Mental Health, Climate & Health** — plus discovery research. *(Wellcome Leap is a separate US-based entity, documented elsewhere.)* Grantees are **major/representative**; some COVID-era totals vary by source.

## Section 1 — Programmes and Initiatives

### 1.1 Infectious Disease challenge (post-2020/21 strategy)
Works with communities affected by escalating infectious disease (incl. drug-resistant infections) to prevent, prepare for, and respond to threats; emphasises epidemic/pandemic preparedness and equitable access. Sub-areas: pandemic preparedness, AMR, surveillance/genomics.

### 1.2 Epidemic & pandemic preparedness
Argues for sustained "between-outbreak" investment; co-founded standing institutions (CEPI, ISARIC, CTA) rather than only reacting.

### 1.3 CEPI (co-founder, 2016–2017)
Concept in a **July 2015 NEJM** paper co-authored by Jeremy Farrar; launched **Jan 2017** at Davos. Wellcome contributed **US$100M** to the ~$460M initial round; Farrar on CEPI's board. **Jan 2022:** Wellcome + Gates **jointly pledged US$300M** for the 100 Days Mission.

### 1.4 COVID-19 Therapeutics Accelerator (2020)
With Gates + Mastercard, **up to $125M** seed (Wellcome **up to $50M**). Initial **$20M (Mar 2020)** to University of Washington, University of Oxford, La Jolla Institute for Immunology.

### 1.5 Broader COVID-19 response & ACT-Accelerator
~US$80M (~£60m) pledged for LMIC COVID treatments/research/capacity (incl. the CTA seed); a further **$100M** pledged for COVID research; co-architect of the ACT-Accelerator (May 2020). *(Cumulative COVID totals vary by source.)*

### 1.6 Drug-resistant infections / AMR
**Drug-Resistant Infections Priority Programme** est. **2016**. Vehicles: **CARB-X** (Boston University-led, co-founded 2016; ~US$155.5M over 5 yrs earlier rounds + a renewed **US$60M** award); **GARDP** (£1M, 2018); **SEDRIC**; **ACORN**; a joint Gram-negative early-discovery initiative with the Novo Nordisk + Gates foundations.

### 1.7 Surveillance & genomics
**COG-UK** (launched 1 Apr 2020, ~£20M from DHSC/NIHR/UKRI + Genome Research Ltd/Sanger; led by Sharon Peacock; +£12.2M late 2020). SARS-CoV-2 genomic surveillance in Africa (KEMRI-Wellcome a regional hub; grant 222574/Z/21/Z); sustained investment underpinning the Africa CDC **Africa Pathogen Genomics Initiative** (~$100M).

### 1.8 Wellcome Sanger Institute
Operated by **Genome Research Limited (GRL)**, a **wholly owned Wellcome subsidiary**; Wellcome has core-funded it in five-year cycles since **1992** (originally for the Human Genome Project). Central to UK/global COVID sequencing (COG-UK).

### 1.9 Clinical research networks — ISARIC & overseas units
**ISARIC** (est. 2011; 70+ networks across ~140 countries) — funded by Wellcome, UK FCDO, and Gates; awards £4.5M (2019) and **up to £16.5M over 5 yrs (Apr 2025)**; Global Support Centre at Oxford's Pandemic Sciences Institute. Plus Wellcome's standing overseas programmes (below).
