# The Rockefeller Foundation

## Introduction
Chartered 1913; one of the oldest/most influential private public-health funders. Its modern pandemic work (data science, pathogen genomic surveillance, early warning) is framed as the "next chapter" in a century-long legacy (hookworm, yellow fever, founding the first schools of public health). Grantees below are **representative major awards**, not exhaustive (no complete public grants ledger; several rockefellerfoundation.org/grant pages 403/404 to fetch).

---

## Section 1 — Programmes

### Historical context
Founding 1913; Rockefeller Sanitary Commission for hookworm (1909–1914, $1M); International Health Division (1913–1951, >$94M, ~80–90 countries); founded Johns Hopkins School of Public Health ($267K, 1916), Harvard SPH ($1.6M), LSHTM ($2M). Max Theiler developed the **17D yellow-fever vaccine** (1937) in the Foundation's lab (1951 Nobel).

### Precision Public Health Initiative ($100M, 2019)
With UNICEF/WHO/Global Fund/World Bank GFF/Gavi — "data for health" origins of the pandemic-era framing.

### $1B COVID-19 recovery commitment (Oct 2020)
Largest single commitment in Foundation history (3 yrs); pillars: green recovery + equitable access to COVID tests/vaccines/data. Financed partly via a first-ever $700M bond. Parent of the PPI and most surveillance/testing work.

### U.S. COVID-19 testing
**National Covid-19 Testing Action Plan** (Apr 21, 2020) — the "1-3-30" plan; Foundation committed **$15M** to launch. **Testing Solutions Group** (May 2020, $5M); K-12 school testing; **STAT** alliance ($30M Advance Market Commitment); **Project ACT** (Jan 2022, initial $7.45M → ~$33.2M, ~4.5M at-home tests).

### Genomic & disease surveillance
"More than $20M" genomic-surveillance package (June 2021); national genomic-surveillance blueprint (recommended sequencing ≥5% of cases); wastewater/environmental surveillance (incl. $3M to Global.health).

### Pandemic Prevention Institute (PPI): rise and closure
Dr. **Rick Bright** joined Mar 2021 to build it; **up to $150M over 3 yrs** announced Oct 25, 2021 (build datasets/analytics for detection/early warning; "stop outbreaks in 100 days"). **Wound down ~September 2022** (Bright departed); work folded into the Foundation (partly its climate strategy). Bright's post-mortem cited philanthropy's "cycles of panic and neglect." *(Date conflict: Sept 2022 best-supported; one pass said 2023.)*

## Section 2 — Grantees and Partners (representative; DISCLOSED/NOT noted)

**Umbrella:** $1B green/COVID recovery (Oct 2020); PPI up to $150M (Oct 2021); ~$20M pathogen-sequencing package (Jun 2021); $55M Global Vaccination Initiative (Apr 2022).

- **Stellenbosch University — CERI** (South Africa): $4.5M initial / $9M total (Jun 2021–Aug 2026); African pathogen genomics (Tulio de Oliveira; central to detecting Omicron).
- **CSIR-CCMB** (Hyderabad, India): $9.5M (2021–2024); India pathogen genomics.
- **Boston Children's + University of Oxford — Global.health**: $3M (Nov 2022).
- **Broad Institute, LSU Health Shreveport, University of Florida**: U.S. Regional Accelerators for Genomic Surveillance (under ~$20M package, not disclosed).
- **Scripps Research** ($446,565, DETECT wearables); **Johns Hopkins Center for Health Security** ($400K); **Harvard Chan** ($249,511); **Helix** (~$898K, single-source).
- **WHO Hub for Pandemic and Epidemic Intelligence** (Berlin): **$5M** (May 2023); plus **data.org** $2.5M (Nov 2023).
- **Africa CDC** (via Africa Public Health Foundation): **$12M** (Jan 2021, PACT testing). *(NOT the $100M Africa Pathogen Genomics Initiative — that's Gates/US CDC.)*
- **PATH/Digital Square** "DRIVE Demand" ($5M, Dec 2022); Climate & Health Data ($1.5M with Wellcome, Dec 2024).
- **The Global Fund** — Data Science Catalytic Fund ($15M RF + $10M, 2019).
- **Pandemic Fund** (World Bank): **up to $15M** pledge (July 2022) — RF a founding philanthropic donor (with Gates/Wellcome).
- **IPSN Catalytic Grant Fund**: ~$2M across 10 projects (Nov 2024), RF one of three funders (with Gates/Wellcome).

## Key flags
PPI closure best dated Sept 2022 (verify Science post-mortem); **Africa PGI $100M is Gates/US CDC, not RF** (common misattribution); single-source figures (Helix ~$898K; $1.49M Baltimore school grant; 5% sequencing target) to re-verify; date-scope notes (Precision Public Health 2019; Project ACT 2022; $1B commitment Oct 2020).
