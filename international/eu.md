# European Union — Institutional Funders of Pandemic Preparedness and Medical Countermeasures

The EU has no single BARDA/NIH/DARPA equivalent; MCM, pandemic-preparedness, and biosecurity functions are distributed across HERA, Horizon Europe, public-private partnerships (IHI/IMI), the EIB, defence instruments, and EU contributions to multilaterals (CEPI, ACT-A). €→USD ~1.08. Representative, not exhaustive; uncertain figures flagged.

## 1. Programmes / Funders

### HERA — European Health Emergency Preparedness and Response Authority (est. Sept 2021)
The EU's closest BARDA analogue: anticipates threats and ensures development/production/procurement/stockpiling of MCMs. Indicative budget **~€6bn for 2022–2027** (blended EU4Health, Horizon Europe ~€1.7bn, rescEU, UCPM). Instruments: **rescEU** medical stockpiles; **EU FAB** "ever-warm" reserved vaccine-manufacturing capacity; **joint procurement** of vaccines/therapeutics/antivirals for Member States; **HERA Invest** (~€110m venture-loan facility under InvestEU, with the EIB; up to 50% of project costs for MCM SMEs).

### Horizon Europe (2021–2027, €95.5bn)
Principal R&I programme. Health = **Cluster 1 (~€8.25bn)**, funding pandemic-preparedness/infectious-disease/MCM research and the **European Partnership on Pandemic Preparedness** (~€63m earmarked 2026–2027). Predecessor **Horizon 2020** ran COVID emergency calls: €48.2m/18 projects (Jan 2020), €128m/24 projects (May 2020), +~€123m variant funding (2021).

### ERC
European Research Council (~€16bn 2021–2027, ~17% of Horizon Europe) — investigator-driven frontier research (basic virology/immunology underpinning pandemic science); context, not a directed MCM funder.

### IHI / IMI
Public-private (Commission + EFPIA/industry). **Innovative Health Initiative (2021–2027) ~€2.4bn** (€1.2bn Horizon Europe + ~€1bn industry + €0.2bn partners). Predecessor **IMI** ran the **Ebola+ programme** (2014–15, ~€280m+ call; ~€140m IMI/H2020 + EFPIA in-kind) and a March 2020 fast-track COVID call (EU €45m→€72m; total **€117m** across 8 projects).

### EIB
EU lending arm financed developers: **BioNTech up to €100m** (June 2020), **CureVac €75m** (July 2020); €230m loan supporting Team Europe/COVAX; now facilitates HERA Invest.

### European Defence Fund (2021–2027)
CBRN strand (detection/identification/monitoring; decontamination); annual work programmes ~€1bn; biodefence is a small dual-use slice.

### EU support to CEPI & ACT-Accelerator
EU joined CEPI in 2019 (co-funds via Horizon Europe; e.g. ~€35m; a 2022 EU+CEPI Rift Valley fever call). Commission launched the **Coronavirus Global Response** pledging marathon (May 2020, raised €7.4bn; Commission pledged €1.4bn); cumulatively €15.9bn pledged for ACT-A; Team Europe committed ~€400m to COVAX.

## 2. Grantees / Awardees (representative)

**EIB / HERA Invest:** BioNTech (up to €100m, 2020); CureVac (€75m, 2020); Leyden Labs (€20m HERA Invest venture debt, June 2025, broadly protective antiviral nasal sprays); **Fabentech** (€20m, *first* HERA Invest agreement, Oct 2024, broad-spectrum biothreat therapeutics); a 2nd HERA Invest agreement (Dec 2024, AMR).

**HERA / EU joint procurement (vaccines & MCMs):** Aug 2020–Nov 2021 — 11 contracts, 8 manufacturers, up to **4.6bn doses, ~€71bn** expected total (European Court of Auditors). Pfizer/BioNTech (up to **2.4bn doses**, incl. the May 2021 900m+900m-option contract — the EU's largest); Moderna, AstraZeneca, Janssen, Sanofi/GSK, CureVac (APAs); Valneva (up to 60m doses VLA2001); **CSL Seqirus** (pre-pandemic H5 avian flu: up to 665,000 doses + 40m option, June 2024); **Bavarian Nordic** (MVA-BN mpox/smallpox: up to 8m doses framework; 175,420 doses donated to Africa CDC, Aug 2024).

**IMI/IHI consortia:** **CARE** (Corona Accelerated R&D in Europe — largest IMI COVID therapeutics consortium); **Ebola+** grantees (EBOVAC, EBODAC, EBOMAN tied to the Janssen Ad26/MVA regimen).

## Key flags
HERA budget cited €5–6bn (varies by source/date); HERA Invest €100m vs €110m; the €71bn / 4.6bn-dose total is the ECA's late-2022 estimate; per-deal vaccine prices largely redacted. Representative — procurement spans many more suppliers/Member-State opt-ins.
