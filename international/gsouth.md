# India, South Africa & Brazil — Global South Government Funders

Government funders of pandemic preparedness, infectious-disease R&D, biosecurity, genomic surveillance, and MCMs across three major Global South vaccine/research powers. Local currency + approx USD (India ~₹75/USD COVID-era; SA ~R17/USD; Brazil ~R$5.3/USD). Representative, not exhaustive; uncertain figures flagged.

## 1. Programmes / Funders

### INDIA
- **DBT** (Department of Biotechnology) — apex biotech-R&D body; parent of BIRAC, THSTI; led COVID vaccine R&D; runs Infectious Disease Biology Program. Budget ~₹3,446 crore (2025–26).
- **BIRAC** (Biotechnology Industry Research Assistance Council, 2012) — DBT's industry–academia funding/de-risking vehicle; implements the missions below.
- **Mission COVID Suraksha** (launched 1 Dec 2020) — India's COVID Vaccine Development Mission, **₹900 crore (~US$120M)** over 12 months via BIRAC; funded ZyCoV-D (DNA), CORBEVAX (subunit), GEMCOVAC-19 (mRNA), iNCOVACC (intranasal).
- **Ind-CEPI Mission** (approved 27 Mar 2019) — "India Centric Epidemic Preparedness through Rapid Vaccine Development," **₹312.92 crore (~US$45M)**, via BIRAC; renewed/broadened (mAbs/Disease X) Sept 2025.
- **ICMR** (Indian Council of Medical Research) — co-developed Covaxin with Bharat Biotech (NIV Pune isolated the strain); 5% royalty on Covaxin.
- **THSTI** — DBT institute (2009); coordinated DBT's COVID consortium; hosts CEPI Centralized Network Lab.
- **National Biopharma Mission ("Innovate in India")** (2017, **US$250M**, 50% World Bank loan) — ~155 projects, 18+ products incl. ZyCoV-D.

### SOUTH AFRICA
- **SAMRC** (South African Medical Research Council, 1969) — statutory funder (~R2bn, 2025, ~US$110M); product-development unit **SHIP** (with DSI). After 2025 US PEPFAR/NIH cuts, an **R600m rescue package** (R400m Treasury + R100m Gates + R100m Wellcome, Aug 2025).
- **DSI** (Department of Science and Innovation) — science ministry; co-funds SHIP, NGS-SA, the mRNA hub; funded KRISP genomics (~R25m of R45m, 2020).
- **NGS-SA** (Network for Genomic Surveillance SA, May 2020) — detected Beta (Dec 2020) and Omicron (Nov 2021).
- **CERI (Stellenbosch)** + **KRISP (UKZN)** — genomic-surveillance centres (Tulio de Oliveira); CERI launched Sept 2021 (anchored by $4.5M Rockefeller + Chan Soon-Shiong).
- **WHO mRNA Technology Transfer Hub at Afrigen (Cape Town)** — WHO + Medicines Patent Pool; ~US$117M programme (US$89M received by Sept 2024); MPP granted **€39M to Afrigen** (2021–2026); funders incl. EU/Team Europe (~€40M), Belgium, Canada, France, Germany, Norway, SA govt/SAMRC. CEPI added US$6.2M (Jan 2025, TB/HIV/RVF).
- **Biovac** (Cape Town, 2003) — public–private vaccine manufacturer; Pfizer/BioNTech fill-finish (2021); first WHO-hub partner to receive the mRNA tech (2024).

### BRAZIL
- **Fiocruz** (Oswaldo Cruz Foundation) — Ministry of Health-linked; 2023 budget R$9.46bn (~US$1.9bn); **Bio-Manguinhos** unit (largest Brazilian immunobiologicals producer; absorbed AstraZeneca/Oxford tech; 153M+ COVID doses).
- **Butantan Institute** (São Paulo, 1901) — state vaccine/antivenom institute; CoronaVac fill-finish/tech transfer with Sinovac.
- **Ministry of Health** — dominant funder; PDP tech-transfer model; CEIS health-industrial strategy (R$42bn to 2026, R$4bn FINEP).
- **FAPESP** (São Paulo Research Foundation, 1962; 1% of state tax; ~R$1.36bn 2023) — funded CoronaVac Phase 3 + ~R$30M emergency COVID lines.
- **FINEP** (federal innovation funder, MCTI) — ~R$752M against COVID; **CNPq** co-funded the R$50M MCTIC/CNPq/MS call (2020).

## 2. Grantees / Awardees (representative)

**India — Mission COVID Suraksha R&D grants (via BIRAC):** Cadila/Zydus (ZyCoV-D, ₹40 crore); Bharat Biotech (Covaxin, ₹23.73 crore); Gennova (mRNA/GEMCOVAC, ₹22.50 crore); Biological E (Corbevax, ₹22.50 crore); THSTI (₹78.96 lakh). Manufacturing augmentation ~₹65 crore each to Bharat Biotech + Haffkine. *(Separately: Biological E got a ₹1,500 crore procurement advance for 30 crore Corbevax doses — not an R&D grant.)*

**South Africa:** MPP → **Afrigen €39M** (2021–26); CEPI → Afrigen **US$6.2M** (2025); Gates → **Biovac US$5M** (2023); DSI → **KRISP R25m** (2020); Rockefeller → **CERI $4.5M** (2021).

**Brazil:** Ministry of Health → **Fiocruz R$1.9bn** (100M AstraZeneca doses, 2020); → **Butantan R$2.6bn** (100M CoronaVac doses, 2021); cumulative pandemic vaccine spend ~R$38bn (650M+ doses). FAPESP funded CoronaVac Phase 3; BNDES added R$386M to Butantan (2024, flu-vaccine plant).

## Key flags
Best-documented: Mission COVID Suraksha (₹900cr, itemized BIRAC grants); Brazil MoH contracts (R$1.9bn AZ, R$2.6bn CoronaVac, ~R$38bn cumulative); WHO mRNA hub (€39M Afrigen). Flags: India IIL/BIBCOL manufacturing amounts uncertain; Biological E ₹1,500cr is procurement not R&D (commonly conflated); SA govt ZAR line-items to mRNA hub largely undisclosed; Brazil AstraZeneca tranches must not be summed; Butantan has no single published annual budget.
