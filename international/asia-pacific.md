# Japan, South Korea & Singapore — Government Funders

Principal government funders of pandemic preparedness, infectious-disease R&D, biosecurity, and MCMs in Japan, South Korea, and Singapore — each rebuilt post-COVID, partly modelled on BARDA/NIH. Local currency + approx USD. Representative, not exhaustive; uncertain figures flagged.

## 1. Programmes / Funders

### JAPAN
- **AMED** (Japan Agency for Medical Research and Development, est. 2015) — "control-tower" medical-R&D funder (~¥166bn/~US$1.1bn total R&D FY2023). CEPI collaboration (June 2023).
- **SCARDA** (Strategic Center of Biomedical Advanced Vaccine R&D, est. 22 Mar 2022, inside AMED) — Japan's BARDA-equivalent for vaccines; **¥150.4bn strategic R&D fund** (+ ¥51.5bn university hubs = ¥201.9bn/~US$1.8bn); aligned to the 100 Days Mission.
- **Vaccine Development Flagship Facilities** — "World-leading Vaccine R&D Centers" (¥51.5bn, FY2022–FY2026): 1 flagship + 4 synergy bases + 6 support institutions.
- **COVID company subsidies** (MHLW production) — Shionogi ¥47.69bn, Takeda ¥30.14bn (Novavax), Daiichi Sankyo ¥29.57bn (mRNA), KM Biologics ¥28.5bn (inactivated).
- **NIID → JIHS** — National Institute of Infectious Diseases merged with NCGM (1 Apr 2025) into the **Japan Institute for Health Security**, Japan's CDC-like body.
- **CEPI/Gavi & GHIT Fund** — Japan a CEPI founding funder (~US$125m) + US$300m (2022); Gavi US$300m (2021–25); **GHIT Fund** (neglected-disease PPP; US$200m to GHIT 3.0, 2023).

### SOUTH KOREA
- **KDCA** (Korea Disease Control and Prevention Agency, from KCDC 12 Sep 2020) — surveillance, quarantine, vaccine R&D; infectious-disease R&D ~KRW 434bn (2024, declining).
- **MOHW / KHIDI** — ~KRW 261.6bn (~US$200m, 2024–2028) for new-drug/vaccine development.
- **K-vaccine / Global Vaccine Hub** (2021) — KRW 2.2tn (~US$1.9bn) by 2025; **K-Bio Vaccine Fund** (2022, KRW 500bn).
- **KDCA mRNA Vaccine Development Support Project** — **KRW 505.2bn (~US$356m), 2025–2028** (licensed mRNA vaccine by 2028; 100–200-day platform).
- **KNIH** (Korea National Institute of Health) under KDCA; National Action Plan on AMR (One Health).

### SINGAPORE
- **NMRC** (National Medical Research Council, 1994, MOH) — competitive medical-research grants (~SGD 95m across 5 Large Collaborative Grants, 2024).
- **PREPARE** (Programme for Research in Epidemic Preparedness and Response) — **SGD 100m / 5 yrs** (RIE2025, launched Nov 2022, hosted by NCID; ED Wang Linfa); 5 Co-operatives + 2 Core programmes.
- **A*STAR** — Infectious Diseases Labs (2021, BSL-3); Bioinformatics Institute hosts GISAID; DxD Hub (cPass, Fortitude RT-PCR).
- **Duke-NUS** — Emerging Infectious Diseases programme (2007 post-SARS; invented cPass).
- **DSO National Laboratories / DMERI** — defence bio research (BSL-4 upgrade at Marina Hill ~end-2025).
- **CEPI/NRF** — Singapore pledged US$15m to CEPI (2022–2026) + US$12m (2027–2030); RIE2025 = SGD 25bn.

## 2. Grantees / Awardees

**Japan — SCARDA hubs (FY2022–FY2026; caps):** University of Tokyo "UTOPIA" (flagship, Kawaoka, ¥7.7bn); Osaka "CAMaD" (¥3.8bn); Chiba "cSIMVa" (¥3.8bn); Nagasaki "DEJIMA" (¥3.8bn); Hokkaido "IVRed" (¥3.8bn). **COVID subsidies:** Shionogi ¥47.69bn, Takeda ¥30.14bn, Daiichi Sankyo ¥29.57bn (+ AMED R&D ¥12.265bn), KM Biologics ¥28.5bn; Shionogi Xocova purchase ~¥100bn. **GHIT** Jan 2025 round (¥2bn/8 projects).

**South Korea:** **SK bioscience** (SKYCovione) — **~US$260m cumulative from CEPI** (Dec 2020 $10m; Mar 2021 ~$26.7m; May 2021 up to $173.4m; Dec 2021 up to $50m) + undisclosed Gates grant; Korea's first domestic COVID vaccine (June 2022). **mRNA project (KRW 505.2bn):** EuBiologics-led consortium + GC Biopharma (Phase 1). IVI–Catholic Univ.–Inventage–Enzynomics (~US$13m ARPA-H mRNA grant).

**Singapore:** **ACM Biolabs** — up to **US$2.87m from CEPI** (May 2025, thermostable mRNA delivery); Duke-NUS (CEPI partnerships, incl. Arcturus mRNA; amounts undisclosed); NMRC 2024 LCG awardees (~SGD 95m: Colo-SCRIPT, TAAP-2, SYMPHONY 2.0, TARIPH, ADEPT — SGD 10–25m each); PREPARE ~91 projects (NCID/NUS/NTU/A*STAR; amounts confidential).

## Key flags
Japanese figures most complete (SCARDA caps, MHLW subsidies, GHIT published). Corrections: only Univ. of Tokyo (UTOPIA) is SCARDA's *flagship* (others are synergy bases); "ARM"/"CREID" are not PREPARE cooperatives. Korea's SK bioscience/CEPI tranches solid; per-team mRNA allocations undisclosed. Singapore PREPARE/NMRC per-project amounts mostly confidential. Single-sourced: Japan's US$125m CEPI founding pledge; JIHS/KNIH/A*STAR/DSO budgets.
