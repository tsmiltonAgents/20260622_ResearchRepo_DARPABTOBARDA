# Germany, France & Netherlands — National Government Funders

Principal national-government funders/instruments for pandemic preparedness, infectious-disease R&D, biosecurity, and MCMs in Germany, France, and the Netherlands. €. Representative, not exhaustive; uncertain figures flagged.

## 1. Programmes / Funders

### GERMANY
- **BMBF** (Federal Ministry of Education and Research) — flagship **COVID-19 vaccine "Sonderprogramm"** (up to **€750M**, June 2020, 2020–2021; funded BioNTech, CureVac, IDT Biologika); later refinanced via NextGenerationEU. Major CEPI donor (~€520M total).
- **Robert Koch Institute (RKI)** — Germany's CDC (under the Health Ministry BMG); surveillance, genomic epidemiology, preparedness; pillar of the WHO Berlin pandemic hub.
- **German Center for Infection Research (DZIF)** — 35 institutions/7 sites; 90% federal/10% Länder; key partner in the EU **European Vaccines Hub (~€102M)**. Planned "National Decade Against Post-Infectious Diseases" (2026–2036, ~€500M, *planned*).
- **DFG** — investigator-led/consortium infection research. **Paul-Ehrlich-Institut** — federal vaccines regulator (enabling, not funding).
- **BMZ/KfW/Gavi** — €400M Gavi COVAX AMC (2022); lead AVMA donor (US$318M, 2024); €600M to Gavi through 2030 (June 2025).

### FRANCE
- **ANR** (Agence Nationale de la Recherche) — main research funder; operator of **France 2030**; ran RA-COVID-19 rapid calls (~234–279 projects, ~€32–36M).
- **INSERM / ANRS-MIE** — ANRS | Maladies Infectieuses Émergentes created 1 Jan 2021 (merger of ANRS + REACTing); directed **~€51M to urgent COVID research** in 2021.
- **Institut Pasteur & Pasteur Network** — private non-profit anchoring ~32–33 institutes in ~25 countries; COVID work drew public + philanthropic support (€1M EIB donation; €2M French foreign-ministry grant for African network).
- **Innovation Santé 2030 / France 2030** — launched June 2021, **>€7.5bn for health innovation** (€1.7bn biomedical research) within the €54bn France 2030; pillars incl. PEPR Biothérapies/Bioproduction and **PREZODE** (zoonosis prevention). Recovery "Resilience/Capacity Building" calls: 123 projects, ~€1bn to reshore health-industrial capacity.
- **Bpifrance** — co-invests in biotech/vaccine firms (specific Valneva amounts unconfirmed).
- *France was NOT a CEPI founding-government donor* (founders: Norway, Japan, Germany, Wellcome, Gates).

### NETHERLANDS
- **ZonMw** — national health-research funder; runs the **Pandemic Preparedness** knowledge programme (One Health) at VWS's request; co-represents NL in the EU BE READY partnership.
- **RIVM** — Dutch CDC-equivalent (under VWS); infectious-disease control/surveillance/preparedness. Aggregate Dutch pandemic-preparedness commitment ~€300M (*aggregate, flagged*).
- **Intravacc** — public vaccine-development institute (Bilthoven, from 1894); became a State-owned B.V. (sole shareholder = Dutch State) on 1 Jan 2021; contested privatisation (2023).

## 2. Grantees / Awardees

**Germany — BMBF Sonderprogramm (2020–2021; the exhaustive awardee set):** **BioNTech €375M** (BNT162b2 scale-up); **CureVac up to €252M** (+ separate €75M EIB loan); **IDT Biologika ~€114M** (Phase I–III + fill-finish).

**France:** **Institut Pasteur**-led CEPI consortium (with Themis, Univ. Pittsburgh) — initial CEPI ~US$4.9M (measles-vectored COVID candidate, Mar 2020); ANR RA-COVID-19 (~€32–36M across 234–279 projects); France 2030 biotherapy awardees illustrative (Egle Therapeutics €9.3M, ImCheck €20.18M); Valneva (EU-supplied VLA2001; French/Bpifrance amount unconfirmed).

**Netherlands:** **NCOH** (Netherlands Centre for One Health) — €2.5M (ZonMw, pandemic preparedness); **BePrepared** consortium — €1.6M (ZonMw, social-science); **CHDR** (Centre for Human Drug Research) — ~€9.5M facility; **Intravacc** — funded directly by the State (no single grant figure).

## Key flags
German Sonderprogramm trio (BioNTech/CureVac/IDT) is the complete awardee set. France/Netherlands lists representative (both distributed hundreds of smaller awards). Corrections: France not a CEPI founding-government donor; DZIF "National Decade" €500M is planned (2026–2036); Dutch ZonMw programme total not pinned to one figure (~€300M aggregate reported); Valneva French funding unconfirmed.
