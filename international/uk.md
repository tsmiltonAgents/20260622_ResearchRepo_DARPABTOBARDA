# United Kingdom — Government & Public Funders of Pandemic Preparedness and Infectious-Disease R&D

The principal UK Government/public funders of pandemic preparedness, infectious-disease and vaccine R&D, biosecurity, and medical countermeasures — UK analogues to the US NIH, BARDA, DARPA and CDC. £→USD at ~1.27. The Wellcome Trust is covered in `foundations/wellcome.md` (context only here). Per-dose vaccine prices were redacted across UK procurement; uncertain figures flagged.

## 1. Programmes / Funders

### UKRI — MRC and BBSRC
**UK Research and Innovation (UKRI)** umbrella for the research councils (~£10bn/yr by 2030). **Medical Research Council (MRC)** funds discovery/translational biomedicine incl. infections & immunity (~£800m/yr); core-funds overseas units (e.g. MRC Unit The Gambia at LSHTM, >£20m). **BBSRC** funds underpinning bioscience (pathogen biology, animal health, vaccine platforms). Joint UKRI/DHSC: UK Vaccine R&D Network (£120m, 2016–2021); GCRF vaccine networks (£9.4m, £6.4m MRC).

### NIHR
**National Institute for Health and Care Research** — DHSC-funded applied clinical/public-health research (England); funds the **Health Protection Research Units (HPRUs)** with UKHSA and major COVID vaccine/therapeutic trials. Latest HPRU round **~£80m** (13 units + focus awards) from 1 Apr 2025 (5 yrs). NIHR overall >£1bn/yr.

### DHSC & UKHSA
**DHSC** leads domestic health protection/preparedness; ran the **UK Vaccine Network** (£134m, 2016–2022; Ebola/Zika/MERS/CCHF) and pledged **£160m to CEPI** over 5 yrs (from 2022, with FCDO). **UK Health Security Agency (UKHSA)** established 1 Apr 2021 (successor to Public Health England + NHS Test and Trace + Joint Biosecurity Centre) — the UK CDC-equivalent; FY2024-25 opex ~£2.29bn. Runs high-containment labs at **Porton Down** and **Colindale**; opened the **Vaccine Development and Evaluation Centre (VDEC)** at Porton Down (Aug 2023); domestic secretariat for the **100 Days Mission** (UK 2021 G7). Planned **National Biosecurity Centre** at Harlow — costs escalated £530m (2015) → ~£3.2bn (NAO 2024); £250m committed July 2025 (final total uncertain).

### UK Vaccine Taskforce (2020) & VMIC
**Vaccine Taskforce (VTF)** — created Apr–May 2020 (chair Kate Bingham; BEIS → DHSC Mar 2021); the UK's Operation Warp Speed analogue. NAO: total vaccine effort up to **£11.7bn**; ~£2.9bn procurement (up to 267m doses across first five contracts), £8.3bn programme funding (£5.6bn spent by Oct 2021). **Vaccines Manufacturing and Innovation Centre (VMIC)**, Harwell — ~£66m (2018) + £131m (May 2020) ≈ £197–215m public; **sold to Catalent (US) Apr 2022** before completion (price undisclosed).

### Dstl, Porton Down
**Defence Science and Technology Laboratory (Dstl)** — MOD executive agency (2001); biological/chemical defence research (UK defence-science lab). FY2024-25 income ~£971m. Identified Novichok (2018 Salisbury); ran the Defence COVID Laboratory. Work is defensive (detection/protection/MCM).

### ARIA
**Advanced Research and Invention Agency** — the UK's ARPA/DARPA, established Jan 2023, **£800m over 2023–2028**. Most pandemic-relevant programme: **Sustained Viral Resilience** (thesis published Aug 2025) — engineering innate immunity for durable broad-spectrum respiratory-virus protection (budget not yet public). Also flagged early biological-threat detection; **Programmable Plants** (£62.4m) is ag-biosecurity/crop resilience.

## 2. Grantees / Awardees (representative)

**Universities:** University of Oxford (Jenner Institute; Pandemic Sciences Institute) — ChAdOx1/AstraZeneca vaccine; ~£65.5m early-manufacturing + ~£2.6m research + £20m trials (May 2020); CEPI Disease X up to $80m; Oxford/Janssen MERS/Lassa/Nipah up to $19m. Imperial College London — saRNA COVID vaccine, >£41m UK gov + ~£5m philanthropic (2020). LSHTM — MRC Unit The Gambia (>£20m); Wellcome Discovery Awards (>£13m, 2025). University of Edinburgh — £4.9m MRC COVID (GenOMICC). Liverpool School of Tropical Medicine — £4.7m (Research England) human-challenge facility.

**Vaccine Taskforce company deals (2020–21; doses, prices redacted):** AstraZeneca/Oxford (100m, non-profit pricing); Valneva (up to 100m; 5-yr deal up to €1.4bn/~$1.65bn; UK terminated Sept 2021, later settled); Pfizer/BioNTech (~100m); Novavax (60m, Teesside); GSK/Sanofi (60m); Moderna (7m → now >£1bn UK R&D/manufacturing partnership); CureVac (~50m; withdrew 2021).

**Health Protection Research Units (2025–2030):** 13 units, ~£80m; Oxford largest (~£11m, HCAI/AMR); most ~£5.5m each (UCL, Leicester, Liverpool, Bristol, LSHTM…).

*Representative, not exhaustive — many smaller MRC/NIHR/BBSRC responsive-mode grants not itemized.*

## Key flags
ARIA Sustained Viral Resilience budget not yet public; "£8.9bn VTF" unverified (use NAO ~£2.9bn procurement / £8.3bn programme / up to £11.7bn total); VMIC 2022 sale price undisclosed; UK Vaccine Network £120m (launch) vs £134m (current); VTF was BEIS-led most of 2020; per-dose prices redacted.
