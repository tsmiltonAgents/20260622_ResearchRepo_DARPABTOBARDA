# Gavi, the Vaccine Alliance & The Global Fund

The two largest multilateral financing vehicles for infectious-disease commodities in low/lower-middle-income countries. Gavi shapes vaccine markets and finances immunisation; the Global Fund grants to HIV/TB/malaria country programmes. Both took central pandemic roles in COVID and have since institutionalised PPR. USD. Representative, not exhaustive; provisional figures flagged.

## 1. Programmes / mechanisms

### Gavi, the Vaccine Alliance
- **Core model** — pools donor money to buy vaccines at negotiated prices and co-finance introduction in low-income countries (rising co-financing until "transition"). >1.2bn unique children immunised since 2000.
- **Replenishments** — *Gavi 5.0 (2021–2025)*: **US$8.8bn** raised (June 2020 summit; UK $2.1bn, US $870m, France $813m, Norway $796m). *Gavi 6.0 (2026–2030)*: need ~US$11.9bn; June 2025 summit secured **>US$9bn** (~21% short) + ~US$4.5bn DFI financing; targets 500M+ children, expanded African manufacturing + stockpiles (mpox, hepatitis E).
- **COVAX** — ACT-A vaccines pillar, co-led by Gavi/CEPI/WHO/UNICEF (UNICEF procurement). The **Gavi COVAX AMC** funded free doses for **92 economies**, backed by **>US$12bn**. Closed 31 Dec 2023.
- **African Vaccine Manufacturing Accelerator (AVMA)** — launched June 2024; **up to US$1.2bn over 10 yrs** (~12 donors); "pull" mechanism: milestone payments (US$10–25M) on WHO prequalification + accelerator payments US$0.30–0.50/dose via UNICEF tenders. Supports AU goal of ~60% African-made vaccines by 2040.
- **Day Zero Financing Facility (DZF) / First Response Fund** — DZF up to **US$2.5bn** surge capacity; First Response Fund up to **US$500M** at-risk financing; used in 2024 to buy mpox vaccines within ~40 days of the PHEIC.

### The Global Fund
- **Core model** — grants to country programmes (Principal Recipients) for HIV/TB/malaria + resilient systems for health (RSSH); >US$5bn/yr.
- **C19RM (COVID-19 Response Mechanism)** — created Apr 2020; primary channel for COVID tests, oxygen, PPE, health-systems. Cumulative **>US$5bn** across 100+ countries (2020–2023): >US$1bn diagnostics, US$767M PPE, US$617M oxygen; +US$867M for PPR (Feb 2023).
- **PPR in strategy** — Strategy 2023–2028 added an "evolving" PPR objective leveraging HIV/TB/malaria + RSSH.
- **Replenishments** — 7th (2023–2025): **US$15.7bn**. 8th (2026–2028): target US$18bn; Nov 2025 Johannesburg summit secured **~US$11.34bn** (~37% short; US pledge reported US$4.6bn — provisional).

## 2. Grantees / awardees

**COVAX / AMC** — delivered **~2bn doses to ~146 economies** (74% of doses to low-income countries; 52 of 92 AMC economies relied on COVAX for >half supply). **UNICEF** procurement agent. Manufacturers incl. AstraZeneca/Serum Institute, Pfizer-BioNTech, Moderna, J&J, Novavax.

**AVMA beneficiaries (forward-looking)** — Biovac & Aspen (South Africa), Institut Pasteur de Dakar (Senegal, MADIBA facility ~300M doses/yr, IFC-backed); EVA Pharma/Biogeneric (Egypt), Afrigen (SA, mRNA). *Pays on prequalification/delivery → pipeline, not confirmed payouts.*

**Global Fund C19RM** — 125 applicants/100+ countries (Principal Recipients = health ministries, UNDP); ~65% via wambo.org pooled procurement. Antigen rapid tests: SD Biosensor Standard Q, Abbott Panbio (part of the 120M-test WHO/CHAI/Africa CDC partnership); oxygen via PSA plants. Per-supplier amounts not itemised.

## Key flags
Gavi 6.0 ~$9bn vs ~$11.9bn need (Phase 6 page stresses mpox/hepatitis E, not the exact "Ebola/mpox/cholera" framing). AVMA beneficiaries are pipeline (payments trigger on prequalification). Global Fund C19RM totals vary by reporting date (approximate). 8th Replenishment ~$11.34bn pledged is provisional. Per-supplier C19RM award amounts not public.
