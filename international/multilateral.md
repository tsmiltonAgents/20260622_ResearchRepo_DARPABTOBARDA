# Multilateral & International Non-Profit Funders / Mechanisms

Principal multilateral and non-profit funders for pandemic preparedness, infectious-disease R&D, and MCMs — **excluding CEPI, Gavi, Global Fund** (covered elsewhere; noted only for context). USD. Representative, not exhaustive; uncertain/dated figures flagged.

## 1. Programmes / Mechanisms

### WHO mechanisms
- **ACT-Accelerator (2020–2023)** — COVID umbrella; 4 pillars: Diagnostics (FIND + Global Fund), Therapeutics (Unitaid + Wellcome), Vaccines/COVAX (CEPI/Gavi/WHO), Health Systems Connector. **~US$24.2bn** cumulative by June 2023; >1.8bn doses to 146 countries, >161M tests, >40M treatment courses, >2bn PPE items.
- **WHO Hub for Pandemic and Epidemic Intelligence** (Berlin, 1 Sept 2021) — ~€90M from Germany; with Robert Koch Institute; collaborative surveillance/early detection.
- **PRET** (Preparedness and Resilience for Emerging Threats, Apr 2023) — transmission-mode lens (Module 1 respiratory); guidance/coordination, not a large grant fund.
- **WHO R&D Blueprint** — priority-pathogen framework (Ebola/Marburg/Lassa/MERS/Nipah/CCHF/RVF/Zika/plague/Pathogen X).
- **WHO BioHub** (2021, with Switzerland; Spiez Laboratory) — biosafe pathogen-material sharing.
- **Pandemic Agreement / PABS** — adopted at the 78th WHA (20 May 2025); PABS (Article 12) annex still under negotiation (extended May 2026).
- **mRNA Technology Transfer Programme** (2021, WHO+MPP; Afrigen hub) — ~US$117M committed (~US$89M received); 14 manufacturing partners.

### The Pandemic Fund (World Bank-hosted, est. 2022)
PPR financing for LMICs; WHO is the most-used implementing entity (not host). **Round 1** (Jul 2023): **US$338M**, 19 projects, 37 countries. **Round 2** (2024): ~US$547M (US$418M/40 countries + US$128.89M fast-tracked for mpox/10 countries). Cumulative through two rounds ~US$885M to 75 countries; later reporting cites ~**US$1.4bn** cumulative incl. Round 3 (flag: later-stage).

### Unitaid
Founded 2006 (Brazil, Chile, France, Norway, UK); WHO-hosted, Geneva. Market-shaping for therapeutics/diagnostics/prevention in LMICs; >US$2.5bn contributions since inception; co-led ACT-A Therapeutics (with Wellcome); ~US$265M for therapeutics/dx/access (2022).

### FIND
Geneva diagnostics PDP (>150 partners); co-led ACT-A Diagnostics (with Global Fund); helped halve rapid-test costs to <US$1; supplied ~80% of tests used in Africa early in COVID; flagged a ~US$6bn diagnostics gap (2021).

### DNDi
Founded 2003 (MSF + national institutes + TDR); neglected-disease R&D; coordinates the **ANTICOV** adaptive platform trial (largest mild/moderate COVID treatment trial in Africa, 13+ countries; fluoxetine+budesonide), funded by Germany's BMBF/KfW + Unitaid + EDCTP + Starr/Stavros Niarchos foundations.

### GARDP
Established 2016 (WHO + DNDi); non-profit antibiotic R&D for AMR. Sept 2024: **10 funders pledged >€60M (~US$66M)**; Germany (BMZ/KfW) largest (~€127.8M total); UK 2nd (+£7M); GSK first pharma funder (~€4.5M).

### GHIC / World Bank-IFC (brief)
**GHIC** manages **BARDA Ventures** (US$50M min / up to US$500M over 10 yrs) — cross-reference (US-anchored). World Bank/IFC provide health-systems/PPR financing and host the Pandemic Fund.

## 2. Grantees / Awardees (representative)

**Pandemic Fund Round 1** (Jul 2023, US$338M, 37 countries, 19 projects) — implementing entities: **WHO (15), FAO (10), UNICEF (8), World Bank (7), IADB (2), ADB (1), AIIB (1)**; recipient countries incl. Bhutan, Burkina Faso, Cambodia, Ethiopia, India, Moldova, Nepal, Paraguay, Togo, Yemen, Zambia (+ regional projects); ~30% to sub-Saharan Africa. **Round 2** US$418M/40 countries + US$128.89M mpox fast-track/10 countries (WHO/UNICEF/Africa CDC/PAHO).

**Unitaid** — recurring grantees: WHO, UNICEF, CHAI, Stop TB Partnership/Global Drug Facility, Global Fund; funded DNDi's ANTICOV + oral-antiviral/oxygen access. **FIND** — >150 partners (TB/mpox/sequencing networks). **DNDi ANTICOV** — 13+ African countries, with ISGlobal + national institutions. **GARDP** — funders/partners incl. Germany, UK, Canada, Japan, Netherlands, Switzerland, EU, GSK, Gates, Wellcome, SAMRC. **mRNA programme** — Afrigen hub (+ SAMRC, Biovac) + 14 LMIC manufacturing partners.

## Key flags
ACT-A ~$24.2bn is cumulative across pillars (overlaps CEPI/Gavi/Global Fund). Unitaid $1.3bn portfolio is a 2019 figure (dated). Pandemic Fund $1.4bn cumulative reflects Round 3 (later-stage). Pandemic Fund per-country amounts not disclosed in announcements. PABS still under negotiation (mid-2026). DNDi annual budget not precisely confirmed.
