# Funders of Global Genomic & Metagenomic Pathogen Biosurveillance

A cross-cutting map of who pays for sequencing-based disease surveillance and early-warning systems. Sequencing-based biosurveillance is funded by a small, interlocking set of actors: US CDC appropriations, a handful of large foundations (Gates, Rockefeller, Wellcome, Open Philanthropy, Chan Soon-Shiong), and the WHO/Africa CDC coordinating bodies. Many awards below are co-funded and recur under multiple primary funders in this project; the focus here is the genomic-surveillance angle, with overlaps flagged. Dollar figures are given where public; several headline totals (e.g. the Africa PGI $100M) never resolve into a clean per-funder breakdown, and those gaps are noted.

## US CDC

**Advanced Molecular Detection (AMD).** Congress established AMD in **FY2014** to fuse next-generation sequencing, bioinformatics, and epidemiology across CDC and partner labs. The base appropriation sat near **$30M** (2014–2020) and grew to roughly **$40–43M/year** by FY2026; ASM's FY2027 advocacy asks for $175M. Most large named recipients (e.g. California's cumulative **$64.9M FY2021–2023**, flowing to UCLA, UCSD, Scripps, LabCorp, Helix; Massachusetts to Broad Institute, Boston University, Theiagen) came from **COVID-19 supplemental** dollars via the ELC cooperative agreement, not the recurring base.

**Pathogen Genomics Centers of Excellence (PGCoE).** Announced **20 Sept 2022**, **$90M over five years** (American Rescue Plan funded) to five health departments partnering with universities: Massachusetts/New England (~$22.2M; Broad, Harvard, BU, Yale), Washington/Northwest (~$18M; UW, Fred Hutch — which also funds Nextstrain, see below), Minnesota (~$18M; U. Minnesota, Mayo), Georgia (~$17M+; UGA, Georgia Tech, Emory), and Virginia ($14.4M; VCU, UVA). Note: this is a **domestic** program — there is no separate US CDC "Global Pathogen Genomics Centers of Excellence"; CDC's international vehicle is the Africa PGI.

**Traveler-based Genomic Surveillance (TGS).** Voluntary, anonymous early-warning sampling of arriving international travelers, run with **Ginkgo Bioworks (Concentric/Ginkgo Biosecurity)**, **XpresCheck (XWELL)**, and Eurofins. A pilot began **Sept 2021** (3 airports: JFK, EWR, SFO). An **Aug 2022** CDC award was valued at **~$16M, with options to exceed $61M** (company/GenomeWeb reporting, not a CDC-quoted document). The program expanded to 30+ pathogens (Nov 2023) and to Miami and Chicago O'Hare (Mar 2024), surpassing **1 million participants by Jan 2026**.

## Rockefeller Foundation

**Pandemic Prevention Institute (PPI).** Announced **25 Oct 2021** with a pledge of **up to $150M**, led by Dr. Rick Bright, to build a global early-warning system. Rockefeller **broke the PPI apart in Sept 2022** after a business analysis showed sustaining the network would cost far more than expected; remaining work became a smaller "Pandemic Prevention Initiative."

**~$20M genomic-surveillance package (June 2021).** Grants plus collaborations across 20+ entities, expanding sequencing in sub-Saharan Africa, India, and the US. Named recipients: **GISAID**, **CSIR–CCMB (India)**, **Stellenbosch University**, and the **US Regional Accelerators for Genomic Surveillance** — the Broad Institute, **LSU Health Shreveport (~$340K)**, and the University of Florida (with UCF and UW–Madison). Per-institution amounts beyond LSU were not disclosed.

**Wastewater.** Rockefeller convened a **Wastewater Action Group** (Fall 2021, 14+ US institutions incl. ASU, Rice, Houston Health Dept, Emory) and issued a Sept 2022 call to scale up monitoring. It profiled **Biobot Analytics**, but no specific Rockefeller→Biobot grant amount is public (collaboration, not a confirmed grant).

**IPSN.** Rockefeller is one of three founding donors to the WHO IPSN catalytic fund (below); its specific share of the $4M is undisclosed.

## Gates Foundation + US CDC: Africa Pathogen Genomics Initiative (Africa PGI)

Launched **12 Oct 2020**, hosted by the **Africa CDC Institute of Pathogen Genomics** (African Union), implemented with ASLM. **$100M over four years.** Disclosed contributions: **Illumina ~$20M in-kind** (confirmed by Illumina), **Gates ~$45M** cash ("catalytic," per GenomeWeb — *not* in the official releases, so secondary), plus in-kind from **Oxford Nanopore** and **Microsoft**. **US CDC is a technical and financial partner but its dollar figure was never publicly disclosed.** The $100M does not resolve into a clean per-funder split. Scale: continental sequencing capacity grew from 7 countries (2019) to ~40 (2024), with 1,000+ experts trained. (Note: the **Global Fund is *not* a founding funder** of Africa PGI — a common misattribution.) On GISAID: no direct Gates→GISAID grant was found; Gates instead backs the open-data alternative PHA4GE.

## Wellcome Trust / Sanger Institute

**COG-UK.** Launched **23 March 2020** with **~£20M** pooled from DHSC, UKRI (NIHR + MRC, grant MC_PC_19027), and Genome Research Limited (Wellcome Sanger Institute); no per-funder split is public. Subsequent DHSC top-ups included **£12.2M (Nov 2020)**, £11.6M (Jan 2021), and £5M (Apr 2021). The Wellcome Sanger Institute was the largest single sequencing hub; COG-UK closed 31 March 2023.

**Africa / global.** Sanger's **Genomic Surveillance Unit (2022–2026)** ran a strategic partnership with **CERI (Stellenbosch, Tulio de Oliveira)** formalized in 2024; a ~R8.7M FCDO figure is single-source/provisional, and there is no standalone dollar-quantified Sanger "SARS-CoV-2-in-Africa" grant. Sanger's firmly funded surveillance work is in **AMR** (GHRU, £6.8M NIHR from 2017; ~£7M renewal 2022) and **malaria** (MalariaGEN/SpotMalaria, funded by Wellcome, Gates, MRC, DFID). Sanger disbanded the GSU in 2026.

## WHO: International Pathogen Surveillance Network (IPSN)

Launched **20 May 2023**, secretariat at the WHO Hub for Pandemic and Epidemic Intelligence (Berlin, German-government supported). A **catalytic grant fund of $4M total** opened **23 Feb 2024**, seeded by **Gates, Rockefeller, and Wellcome** (no per-donor split disclosed; Institute of Philanthropy joined 2025), fiduciary-hosted by the UN Foundation, with grants of $50K–$250K to LMIC members. **First round: ~$2M to 10 projects (26 Nov 2024)** — institutions in Angola, Brazil, Cameroon, DRC, Ghana (Noguchi), India, Laos, Lebanon (wastewater), Rwanda (RBC), and Sri Lanka. A second round (~$1.8M) launched 17 April 2026. WHO's **Global Genomic Surveillance Strategy 2022–2032** (released 30 Mar 2022) frames the 10-year goal: all 194 Member States with timely sequencing access.

## Global Fund / Africa CDC

No source confirms a discrete Global Fund grant to Africa CDC earmarked for sequencing. Money flows indirectly via **C19RM and country grant cycles** (e.g. a Feb 2023 ~$867M pandemic-preparedness tranche; its one named sequencing line, $30M, was Indonesia, not Africa). An Africa CDC–Global Fund MoU (May 2026) covers labs/surveillance broadly without a dollar figure. Treat any "Global Fund $X for African sequencing" claim as unverified.

## Wastewater surveillance funders

CDC's **National Wastewater Surveillance System (NWSS)** launched **Sept 2020**, funded primarily by **$384M (American Rescue Plan)** plus other COVID relief (>$500M total, 2021–2024). Contractors: **Biobot Analytics** (contract 75D30122C13531, ~$31.1M obligated, from Apr 2022) and, after a 2023 recompete, **Verily Life Sciences** (**$38M**, up-to-5-year, Sept 2023, WastewaterSCAN platform); LuminUltra held an earlier contract (amount not public). Rockefeller's wastewater work (above) is the main philanthropic complement.

## Philanthropy / data infrastructure

**Open Philanthropy → Nucleic Acid Observatory (NAO) / SecureBio.** Pathogen-agnostic **metagenomic** early detection of novel/engineered pathogens via wastewater, founded 2021 (Kevin Esvelt/SecureBio; now "SecureBio Detection"). Open Phil grants total **~$9.4M** across NAO program (~$3.43M), Pathogen Early Warning (~$570K), general support (~$4M), and biosecurity research (~$1.42M); some figures are secondary-sourced (OP rebranded to Coefficient Giving in late 2025, breaking live pages).

**CZ Biohub / CZ ID** (formerly IDseq; *cross-referenced elsewhere*). Free open-source **metagenomic NGS** platform from Joe DeRisi's UCSF lab, built by CZ Biohub/CZI (launched 2016 on a $600M commitment) with Gates Foundation co-funding for global expansion; no platform-specific figure is public.

**Chan Soon-Shiong → CERI (Stellenbosch).** The Chan Soon-Shiong Family Foundation is a co-founder of **CERI** (with Stellenbosch and UKZN), providing core founding funding and donating Illumina NovaSeq/NextSeq sequencers (with Illumina and Rockefeller). Distinct from Patrick Soon-Shiong's separate ~$195M **NantSA** vaccine plant (Jan 2022) — do not conflate.

**GISAID.** Controlled-access viral-genome platform (EpiFlu, EpiCoV), historically German-government hosted (BMEL/BLE/Friedrich-Loeffler-Institute). Funders include US CDC/HHS (2007–08), **Rockefeller ($5.2M, 2021–2024)**, and **Singapore (SGD 15M, 2025–2030)**. Governance has drawn sustained controversy (Science investigations).

**Nextstrain.** Open-source real-time pathogen phylogenetics (Trevor Bedford/Fred Hutch + Richard Neher/Basel, since 2015). Funders: the **Open Science Prize ($230K, 2017)**, core **NIH/NIGMS R35 GM119774 (2016–2026)**, **CDC** (Northwest PGCoE), **Gates (INV-018979, Nextstrain for Africa PGI, 2020–2024)**, Templeton, HHMI, and a Wellcome collaborative award. **No evidence Open Philanthropy funds Nextstrain** was found despite searching — flag as a likely-incorrect common assumption.
