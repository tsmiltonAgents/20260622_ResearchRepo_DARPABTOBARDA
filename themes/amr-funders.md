# Global Funders of Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) / Antibiotic R&D

Cross-cutting map of who pays to develop new antibiotics, AMR diagnostics, and stewardship — a few pooled "push" mechanisms, one large "pull"-oriented VC fund, and emerging government subscription models. Several funders recur elsewhere in this project (Gates, Wellcome, Novo Nordisk Foundation, EIB) — focus here is the AMR angle, overlaps flagged. Representative, not exhaustive; uncertain figures flagged.

## CARB-X (push accelerator, early-stage)
Combating Antibiotic-Resistant Bacteria Biopharmaceutical Accelerator (2016), at **Boston University** (Kevin Outterson). Funds pre-clinical→Phase 1 antibacterials/vaccines/diagnostics. Anchor funders **BARDA + Wellcome** (renewed up to **$370M, 2022**), plus Germany BMBF (€41M, 2023), UK GAMRIF (£24M), Novo Nordisk Foundation ($25M, 2023), Italy ($21M), Canada, + Wellcome $60M (2026); Gates a 2016 founder; NIAID in-kind. **$361M across 92 projects (2016–2021)** (~$480M+ cumulative committed). Awardees: Entasis (~$3.8M), Macrolide Pharmaceuticals (up to $6.8M), Forge Therapeutics (~$8.8M), QuantaMatrix ($2.85M, 2025).

## GARDP (push, late-stage + access)
Global Antibiotic R&D Partnership (2016, WHO+DNDi; Swiss foundation 2018). Sept 2024: 10 funders pledged **>€60.8M (~$66M)** — Germany (largest, +€11M), Japan, Monaco, Netherlands, Switzerland, UK, EU/HERA (€20M), GSK (€4.5M), Gates ($7.4M), SAMRC; plans €220M/5yr. Products: cefiderocol access license with Shionogi (135 countries, 2022); **zoliflodacin (NUZOLVENCE)** single-dose oral gonorrhea antibiotic with Entasis/Innoviva — **FDA approved 12 Dec 2025**.

## AMR Action Fund (pull-oriented VC)
Launched July 2020; raised **~$1B** to bring 2–4 antibiotics to patients by 2030. Backers: 20+ pharma (Pfizer, Merck, GSK, J&J, Roche, Novartis, Takeda, Shionogi, Daiichi Sankyo…), EIB, Novo Nordisk Foundation, Wellcome. Portfolio (amounts undisclosed): Adaptive Phage Therapeutics + Venatorx (first, Apr 2022), BioVersys (IPO Feb 2025), Vedanta, Utility Therapeutics (Pivya, FDA-approved Apr 2024).

## Novo Nordisk Foundation: REPAIR + Gr-ADI
**REPAIR Impact Fund** ($165M/DKK 1B, 2018, via Novo Holdings) — 12 companies (Entasis, Spero, Curza, Polyphor, MinervaX…); now closed. **Gr-ADI (Gram-Negative Antibiotic Discovery Innovator)** — Gates + Wellcome + NNF, managed by RTI International; **$50M launch (Feb 2025)** → **$60M to 18 projects in 17 countries (Jan 2026)**. Two distinct events; per-funder splits undisclosed.

## US government
**BARDA** principal late-stage funder (co-founded CARB-X, initial $200M + up to $300M for 2022–2032). **NIAID/NIH** basic/translational AMR (~$565M FY2027 House bill). **PASTEUR Act** (proposed subscription "pull," $75–300M/yr/drug; reintroduced H.R. 7352, Feb 2026, $6B) — never received a floor vote. DoD/DTRA biodefense-adjacent antibacterials.

## UK government
**GAMRIF** (Global AMR Innovation Fund, DHSC ODA) — up to £39M (incl. £24M CARB-X, £5M GARDP). **NHS/NICE "Netflix model"** subscription pilot (2022) — fixed annual fee per antibiotic delinked from volume (cefiderocol, ceftazidime-avibactam; up to £20M/yr; ~£1.9B/16-yr tender 2024). **Fleming Fund** (£265M, 2015, LMIC AMR surveillance) — **being wound down (July 2025)** amid aid cuts. *(Wellcome is a charity, not UK government.)*

## Wellcome Trust
Drug-Resistant Infections programme — **>£300M since 2004** (incl. £175M/5yr, 2017); co-founded CARB-X ($155.5M + ~$60M later), co-funds GARDP + Gr-ADI; set up **SEDRIC** (AMR surveillance, 2018).

## EU / IHI
**ND4BB** (New Drugs for Bad Bugs, ~€700M from 2012; incl. ENABLE Gram-negative platform ~€101M). Current **AMR Accelerator** (~€480M, 2019; 9 projects, 98 partners) — drug-resistant TB, NTM, Gram-negatives.

## Coordinating / surveillance bodies
**Global AMR R&D Hub** (Berlin, G20, 2018; tracks AMR R&D investment); **Fleming Fund** (UK, LMIC surveillance, winding down); **ICARS** (Copenhagen, est. 2018/19, Danish-gov + NNF-funded, LMIC implementation research).

## Key flags
Gr-ADI is "Discovery Innovator"; $50M (2025 launch) and $60M (2026 awards) are separate. News caught: NUZOLVENCE FDA approval Dec 2025; Fleming Fund wind-down July 2025; PASTEUR Act still unvoted. CARB-X $361M disbursed vs $480M+ committed. Per-deal amounts (AMR Action Fund, REPAIR) and Gr-ADI per-funder splits undisclosed; US appropriations are requests/pending.
