# Theme: Respiratory PPE, Air Purifiers, Germicidal UV, and Glycol Air Disinfection

> Cross-cutting thematic file on funding for **airborne-transmission MITIGATION** technologies — respiratory PPE (N95/elastomeric respirators), air purifiers/filtration (HEPA/Corsi-Rosenthal), germicidal ultraviolet (UVGI / upper-room / **far-UVC 222 nm**), and **glycol-vapor air disinfection** (triethylene/propylene glycol). This complements the agency/foundation files, which are organized by funder rather than by technology.

**Framing facts:** (1) The funders who moved first on airborne-disease engineering controls were not the traditional global-health giants but **biosecurity/effective-altruism-aligned philanthropy** (Open Philanthropy/Coefficient, Blueprint Biosecurity) and **crypto-philanthropy** (Balvi/Vitalik Buterin). (2) Glycol air disinfection has 1940s–50s roots (Robertson on triethylene glycol; Puck on propylene/triethylene glycol aerosol microbiology) and is now being revived. (3) Big traditional foundations are largely **absent** from indoor-air *disinfection* (Rockefeller funds air *monitoring*/outdoor air; Gates is essentially not in this space — the foundational 2018 far-UVC paper was funded by the Shostack Foundation + NIH, **not** Gates).

## Part A — Philanthropic & non-governmental funding

### Open Philanthropy / Coefficient Giving (with Good Ventures)
Dominant institutional funder of far-UVC and next-gen PPE in the West. This work lives inside Biosecurity & Pandemic Preparedness under the internal label **"physical transmission suppression"** (far-UVC, upper-room UVGI, ventilation, filtration) plus an adjacent next-gen-PPE sub-area. Staff: Andrew Snyder-Beattie (Director, BPP), Chris Bakerlee (far-UVC). In 2025 issued an **RFI on germicidal far-UVC** (sustained funding appetite). *Caveat: the often-quoted "$200M air-safety program / 68% transmission reduction" comes from an external Cause Exploration Prize essay, NOT an Open Phil commitment.*

### Blueprint Biosecurity — the ecosystem builder
Independent 501(c)(3), founded **2023** by Jake Swett. Three program areas: **far-UVC**, **next-gen PPE**, and **glycol-vapor air disinfection** (the "AIR" — Airborne Infection Resilience — umbrella). Flagship publications: the **"Blueprint for Far-UVC"** (~266 pp, 2025) and a **PPE Blueprint** (respirator stockpiling/surge manufacturing). Funding it receives (~$2.25M identified): Open Phil/Coefficient $900K (Oct 2024) + ~$950K (2023, LongtermWiki); Vanguard Charitable $250K (2024); Founders Pledge ~$150K. It **re-grants** to far-UVC and glycol researchers.

### Balvi Filantropic Fund (Vitalik Buterin crypto-philanthropy)
Largest crypto-philanthropic backer of germicidal-UV and clean-air work; publishes no public grant list. Related vehicles: CryptoRelief, Kanro. (Grant-level detail below.)

### Founders Pledge (GCR Fund) — frequently mis-attributed to Open Phil
Made the **$539,800 grant to JHU Center for Health Security** (Next-Gen PPE in the SNS) and **$84,000 to Blueprint** (Singapore PPE policy workshop). **Not Open Phil.**

### The Shostack Foundation / G. Lynn Shostack
Original philanthropic seed of academic far-UVC — funded David Brenner's Columbia work before government/commercial money. **No dollar amount published.**

### Rockefeller & Gates — largely absent from indoor-air disinfection
Rockefeller funds indoor air *surveillance* (pathogen monitoring via Regional Accelerators for Genomic Surveillance; e.g. UW–Madison air-sampling) and outdoor/climate air (Clean Air Task Force $250K) — **no indoor air *disinfection* grant found**. Gates: **none found** for far-UVC/indoor air/ventilation/filtration (verified); the 2018 far-UVC paper was Shostack + NIH funded.

## Part B — U.S. Government funding

Two complementary levers: **respiratory protective equipment** (source protection) and **clean air** (ventilation, filtration, air disinfection). Recipients/amounts below are the largest/best-documented, not exhaustive.

### NIOSH / NPPTL (within CDC)
NIOSH is the sole federal respirator certifier (42 CFR Part 84, finalized 1995); its **National Personal Protective Technology Laboratory (NPPTL)** (Pittsburgh) tests/approves respirators and researches improved ones. Made 1,776 approval decisions 2021–2023 (self-reported). Co-led **Project BREATHE** (VA-initiated 2008; 28 consensus recommendations for an ideal healthcare respirator) — feeding work on **reusable elastomeric respirators** and **PAPRs** (interim final rule Apr 14, 2020). During COVID it more than doubled approvals and provided the basis for releasing past-shelf-life SNS respirators.

### BARDA / ASPR — respiratory PPE, the stockpile, DPA Title III, Battelle
The 2020 SNS PPE shortage traced to un-replenished 2009 H1N1 stocks; entering COVID the SNS held only **12.6M N95s + 30.8M surgical masks** (vs up to 3.5B N95s estimated need; GAO-23-106210). HHS obligated **$6.1B in COVID-relief for SNS supplies (FY20–21): $2.4B PPE, $3.0B ventilators**; N95 inventory grew to 626M by Feb 2022.
**DPA Title III** drove **222 investments ~$3.2B across ≥182 companies** (GAO-21-108); ~$279M specifically for N95 capacity — biggest: **3M ($126.0M + $76.0M)**, O&M Halyard/Owens & Minor ($29.3M), Honeywell (~$27.3M), Moldex-Metric ($20.1M); filter media: Lydall ($13.5M), Hollingsworth & Vose (~$1.9M+$2.5M).
BARDA's respirator role was R&D: **Applied Research Associates ~$1.97M** (Oct 2017) → NIOSH-approved ElastoMaskPro (2022); DRIVe Mask Innovation Challenge ($400K Phase 2 prize).
Largest single respiratory contract — **mask decontamination**: DLA (for HHS/FEMA) awarded **Battelle a $415M ceiling contract Apr 13, 2020** (the widely-cited "$400M") for 60 Critical Care Decontamination Systems; demand never materialized (Battelle invoiced ~$150M, ~5M masks decontaminated, ceased Mar 2021, FDA authorization revoked May 2021).

### ARPA-H — BREATHE program
**BREATHE** ("Building Resilient Environments for Air and Total HEalth"), launched **Apr 10, 2024** (PM Jessica Green): platforms that continuously monitor indoor air and trigger interventions (ventilation, filtration, germicidal UV). **Up to $156M / 5 yrs**; teams announced Sep 29, 2025. Four TA1 biosensor performers (each ~$39–40M, from awardee releases): **Mayo Clinic, Poppy Health, SafeTraces, Virginia Tech** (with WashU St. Louis). Target: cut facility-related respiratory illness ≥25%.

### CDC + EPA — Clean Air in Buildings Challenge & ESSER
EPA + CDC + DOE launched the **Clean Air in Buildings Challenge (Mar 17, 2022)** under the National COVID-19 Preparedness Plan — guidance to upgrade to **MERV-13** filters, add **portable HEPA** cleaners, target **5 air changes/hour**. Funding mostly via **ESSER** education-relief (~$190B total; $122B ARP ESSER III) — a USGBC/Center for Green Schools analysis found **air filtration/HVAC ~$5.5B** was the 2nd-highest planned ESSER III category (~500 districts planning >$1M/school).

### EPA — air-sanitizer pathway & Grignard Pure (glycol)
EPA regulates antimicrobial **air sanitizers** under FIFRA. Continuous anti-SARS-CoV-2 air treatment went via **FIFRA Section 18 emergency exemption**: **Jan 15, 2021**, EPA granted emergency exemptions to **Georgia and Tennessee** for **Grignard Pure**, a **triethylene-glycol (52.25%)** product assessed as inactivating 98% of airborne SARS-CoV-2 (extended to MD, NV, PA, TX July 2021). No public dollar figures. Rests on 1940s science (Oswald Robertson, U Chicago — glycol vapors killing airborne bacteria/influenza).

### NIH — germicidal UV (UVGI) air disinfection
Mainly via NIOSH and NIAID. **Far-UVC (222nm):** NIOSH R01 OH012312 to David Welch/Columbia (~$658K/yr, FY2022–25); NIAID STTR AI125006 to Eden Park Illumination (David Brenner co-PI). **Upper-room UVGI for TB:** Edward Nardell (Brigham/Harvard), NIOSH R01 OH009050 (FY2006–15, ~$3.46M; + Fogarty), underpinning a South Africa trial (~80% TB-infection reduction).
