# USAID — U.S. Agency for International Development

## Introduction
For ~two decades USAID was one of the world's largest funders of global pandemic preparedness and emerging-infectious-disease (EID) surveillance, distinguished by a **"One Health"** model — detecting novel pathogens at the animal-human interface before spillover — anchored by the **Emerging Pandemic Threats (EPT)** program (PREDICT) and later **STOP Spillover**. **This apparatus was dismantled in 2025:** following EO 14169 (Jan 20, 2025) and stop-work orders, ~83% of programs were cancelled and USAID's functions were folded into the State Department effective **July 1, 2025**.

**Premise corrections established in research:** STOP Spillover's prime is **Tufts University**, not Tetra Tech (a sub-partner); **DAI was not in STOP Spillover** (it led the earlier EPT RESPOND and Preparedness & Response projects). The EcoHealth→Wuhan controversy primarily concerns an **NIH** grant, not USAID (USAID's PREDICT WIV sub-agreement was a separate, far smaller ~$1.1M, 2009–2019).

## Section 1 — Programmes

### 1.1 Emerging Pandemic Threats (EPT)
Launched 2009 (75% of emerging human diseases are zoonotic). **EPT-1 (2009–2014):** PREDICT, RESPOND, IDENTIFY, PREVENT. **EPT-2 (2014–2019/2020):** PREDICT-2, One Health Workforce (U Minnesota), Preparedness & Response (DAI). No clean single EPT total (the "$1.1B since 2009" is broader GHS portfolio).

**PREDICT (2009–2019):** lead **UC Davis One Health Institute** (PI Jonna Mazet), consortium with EcoHealth Alliance, Metabiota, Wildlife Conservation Society, Smithsonian. Funding (USAspending, to UC Davis): PREDICT-1 ~$74.7M; PREDICT-2 ~$137.1M obligated ($100M announced ceiling); ≈$211.8M combined; + $2.26M COVID extension (Apr 2020). Detected >900 (~949) novel viruses (~60–65 novel coronaviruses peer-reviewed). **Non-renewal at end of term Sept 30, 2019** (framed by press as a "shutdown").
- **RESPOND** (EPT-1): One Health workforce; implementer **DAI**.
- **IDENTIFY** (EPT-1): lab networks/diagnostics; implementers **WHO, FAO, OIE**.
- **PREVENT** (EPT-1): behavioral risk reduction; implementer **AED** (→ FHI 360 after AED dissolved 2011), with Global Viral Forecasting + Metabiota.
- *Successor note:* **DEEP VZN** (~$125M, Washington State University, Oct 2021) — **cancelled July 2023** over biosafety concerns.

### 1.2 STOP Spillover (2020–2025)
Announced Sept 30, 2020; **$100M, 5-yr** (FAIN 7200AA20CA00032). Prime: **Tufts University**; Tetra Tech ARD a sub-partner; other partners JSI, Broad Institute, AFROHUN/SEAOHUN, UCLA, U Glasgow, UNMC, U Washington, icddr,b. ~$31.2M obligated; ~$9.4M terminated in 2025. Countries: Bangladesh, Cambodia, Côte d'Ivoire, Liberia, Sierra Leone, Uganda, Vietnam.

### 1.3 Global Health Security (GHS) program / GHSA role
USAID's niche in the Global Health Security Agenda: One Health, zoonotic risk, lab/surveillance, workforce, AMR. "More than $1.1B since 2009." Also IDDS (Infectious Disease Detection and Surveillance, ICF, ~$120M, 2018–2024). GAO-21-359: USAID obligated ~$655M / disbursed ~$500M for GHS FY2015–2019 (CDC similar). *(The "Ebola supplemental ~$1B for GHS" is a conflation; the GHS-designated line was ~$597M within CDC's share.)*

### 1.4 PIOET / Infectious Disease line
**Pandemic Influenza and Other Emerging Threats (PIOET)** funded H5N1 prep (since 2005) + EPT; relabeled "Global Health Security (formerly PIOET)" from FY2016. Pre-pandemic base ~$50–72M/yr; COVID-era spikes (FY2021 ~$440M, FY2022 ~$700M, FY2023 ~$900M).

### 1.5 COVID-19 international response
Of ~$19.03B appropriated for international COVID components, **~$10.54B managed by USAID** (~$9.81B obligated by Mar 2022). $4B to Gavi COVAX AMC (largest single donor). **Global VAX** (Dec 2021, ~$1.6B USAID portion) in ~125 countries.

### 1.6 Antimicrobial resistance
Via **Management Sciences for Health**: MTaPS (2018–2023) and predecessor SIAPS (46 countries). *(The Fleming Fund is a UK program, not USAID.)*

### 1.7 The 2025 dissolution
EO 14169 (Jan 20); Rubio stop-work order (Jan 24); 83% of programs cancelled (Mar 10); dissolution into State notified (Mar 28); **functions to State July 1, 2025**. STOP Spillover terminated. A *Lancet* analysis projected >14M additional deaths by 2030 from USAID defunding.

## Section 2 — Grantees / Awardees (representative)

- **UC Davis (One Health Institute)** — PREDICT prime, ≈$211.8M combined + $2.26M COVID extension.
- **EcoHealth Alliance** — PREDICT consortium partner; ~$1.1M USAID PREDICT WIV sub-agreement (2009–2019), ~$2.5M more (2011–2021). *(Debarment/gain-of-function findings concern the NIH grant R01AI110964, not USAID; HHS debarred EcoHealth + Daszak for 5 years, Jan 17, 2025.)*
- **Metabiota** — PREDICT/PREVENT data analytics (no published USAID share).
- **Wildlife Conservation Society**, **Smithsonian Institution** — PREDICT wildlife surveillance partners (shares unpublished).
- **Tufts University** — STOP Spillover prime ($100M, ~$31.2M obligated).
- **Tetra Tech ARD** — STOP Spillover sub-partner.
- **DAI** — RESPOND (EPT-1) + Preparedness & Response (EPT-2, 16 countries). Not in STOP Spillover.
- **Jhpiego/Johns Hopkins** — ~$159M USAID FY2024 (6th-largest).
- **FHI 360** — ~$474.4M FY2024 (2nd-largest); leads EpiC (GHS-relevant).
- **RTI International** — ~$223.9M FY2024 (3rd-largest); NTDs + surveillance.
- **Chemonics** — GHSC-PSM (~$9.5B ceiling; $6.72B obligated; COVID PPE/test/oxygen logistics).

## Key uncertainties
No clean EPT total; PREDICT ~$211.8M obligated (journalistic "$200M/$207M" round the two-phase total); virus counts vary (don't merge peer-reviewed vs legacy); Ebola "~$1B GHS" is a conflation; EcoHealth debarment = NIH not USAID; Tufts (not Tetra Tech) is STOP Spillover prime; many sub-shares unpublished; usaid.gov offline post-dissolution (figures via USAspending/GAO/KFF/court records).
