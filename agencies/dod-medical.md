# DoD Medical & Biodefense Research Organizations (excl. DARPA, DTRA, JPEO-CBRND)

## Introduction
DoD runs one of the world's largest sustained infectious-disease and MCM research programs, motivated by **force health protection**. Work is performed by Army/Navy labs, funded through the Defense Health Program (DHP) and increasingly coordinated under the Defense Health Agency (DHA) since a 2017–2024 reorganization. Seven nodes: USAMRIID, USAMRDC/MIDRP, CDMRP, WRAIR, NMRC/NAMRU, DHA, GEIS/AFHSD. **No clean research-specific budgets are public** for most (subsumed in DHP RDT&E); awardees are **representative**. No classified material accessed.

## Section 1 — Programmes / Organizations

### 1.1 USAMRIID — U.S. Army Medical Research Institute of Infectious Diseases
Lead DoD lab for medical biological defense; **only DoD lab with BSL-4 containment**; CDC LRN national lab. Established **1969** (succeeding USAMU, 1956), coinciding with Nixon's renunciation of offensive bioweapons. Fort Detrick, MD; ~650–900 staff. Contributions: one of three ZMapp Ebola mAbs (licensed to Mapp, 2009); Ebola Zaire RT-PCR (EUA 2014); tecovirimat/TPOXX Animal Rule studies (with NIAID + SIGA); rF1-V plague vaccine. **2019 research pause**: CDC suspended select-agent registration Jul 2019 (wastewater decon issues); fully reinstated 27 Mar 2020 (no release found).

### 1.2 USAMRDC / MIDRP
USAMRDC = Army medical materiel developer; HQ Fort Detrick; redesignated from USAMRMC **31 May 2019**. Subordinate labs: USAMRIID, WRAIR, USAMRICD, USARIEM, USAISR, USAARL, CDMRP, plus overseas AFRIMS (Thailand), USAMRD-Africa (Kenya), USAMRD-Georgia. ~6,000–7,100 personnel. **MIDRP (Military Infectious Diseases Research Program)** plans DoD MCM solutions (malaria, HIV, dengue/arboviruses, enteric, MDR organisms, emerging infections); executed via WRAIR (Army) and NMRC (Navy); ~$70M/yr appropriated (likely dated).

### 1.3 CDMRP — Congressionally Directed Medical Research Programs
Funds congressionally directed, peer-reviewed biomedical research; managed by USAMRDC. Originated **FY1992** (breast cancer); two-tier review. Cumulative ~**$20B** since 1992 (~$21.6B through FY26). **Peer Reviewed Medical Research Program (PRMRP)**: ~$4.71B cumulative; infectious-disease topics peaked FY16 (AMR, Emerging Infectious Diseases, Hepatitis B, Influenza, Malaria, TB, Vaccine Development); recent cycles narrower (FY25 added TB, Tick-borne Disease, long COVID). **Tick-Borne Disease Research Program (TBDRP)** est. FY2016 (~$62M cumulative through FY26). No standalone AMR program (PRMRP topic only).

### 1.4 WRAIR — Walter Reed Army Institute of Research
**Largest biomedical research lab in DoD.** Descends from the Army Medical School (1893); renamed WRAIR 1953; Silver Spring, MD (with NMRC). Centers: Infectious Disease Research, Military Psychiatry & Neuroscience, Enabling Capabilities (DoD's only cGMP Pilot Bioproduction Facility). **Malaria:** RTS,S with GSK (from 1984; WHO-recommended Oct 6, 2021); next-gen FMP013 with ALFQ adjuvant; CHMI challenge model. **HIV:** MHRP led **RV144** (Thai trial, 31.2% efficacy — only HIV vaccine to show significant efficacy; NEJM Dec 2009). **COVID:** **SpFN (Spike Ferritin Nanoparticle)** pan-coronavirus candidate with ALFQ (Phase 1 from Apr 2021, made in-house). **Dengue** and the ALF/ALFA/ALFQ adjuvant family.

### 1.5 NMRC / NAMRU Labs (Navy)
NMRC performs Navy/Marine biomedical research; traces to NMRI (1942); **renamed "Naval Medical Research Command" 2023**; Forest Glen Annex, MD; under BUMED. ~1,400–1,600+ personnel. **NAMRU network** (renamed 2023): NAMRU INDO PACIFIC (Singapore), NAMRU EURAFCENT (Sigonella, Italy + Cairo/Accra/Djibouti), NAMRU SOUTH (Lima, Peru), NAMRU-Dayton (aerospace), NAMRU-San Antonio (combat casualty). Navy lineage: whole-sporozoite malaria (PfSPZ with Sanaria); Enteric Diseases Dept (ETEC, Shigella, Campylobacter vaccines).

### 1.6 Defense Health Agency (DHA)
Activated **1 Oct 2013**; manages the MHS (~9.5M beneficiaries, ~700+ facilities). FY2017/FY2019 NDAAs directed DHA R&D and transfer of USAMRDC to DHA (R&D workforce "welcome" event May 2024). DHA research = "more than 1,500" staff across five areas including **military infectious diseases**. Line-item budgets not public.

### 1.7 GEIS & AFHSD
**GEIS (Global Emerging Infections Surveillance)** est. **1997** (PDD NSTC-7); only DoD organization coordinating global EID surveillance; joined AFHSC 2008, DHA Public Health 2015. **~$60M distributed to six overseas labs in FY2019**; reaches 70–80+ countries; sequenced SARS-CoV-2 during COVID. **AFHSD** (Armed Forces Health Surveillance Division; AFHSC created 2008 → branch → division): runs the Defense Medical Surveillance System and the DoD Serum Repository (~50–60M specimens).

## Section 2 — Grantees / Awardees / Collaborators (representative)

### 2.1 Intermediary foundations (cross-cutting)
**Henry M. Jackson Foundation (HJF)** — dominant non-profit partner for WRAIR (MHRP, SpFN, malaria) and NMRC; holds NIAID UM1AI108568 and DoD cooperative agreement W81XWH-18-2-0040 (umbrella under which SpFN was executed in part). **CAMRIS International**, **Geneva Foundation** — military-medicine research-support intermediaries.

### 2.2 WRAIR vaccine partners
GSK (RTS,S malaria; dengue LAV/PIV via Army-GSK CRADA); PATH Malaria Vaccine Initiative (RTS,S, Gates-funded >$200M incl. $107.6M 2005); Sanofi Pasteur + VaxGen/GSID (RV144); MHRP overseas partners (KEMRI Kenya, Makerere Uganda, NIMR/Mbeya Tanzania, Nigeria); Aldevron (SpFN GMP plasmid); Fiocruz (dengue).

### 2.3 NMRC / Navy partners
Sanaria (PfSPZ, with U Maryland CVD); Johns Hopkins (Shigella challenge); NIH/NIAID + Cincinnati Children's (Campylobacter); Mount Sinai/Princeton/Flatiron (CHARM COVID analysis); host-nation partners (Peru, Egypt, Indo-Pacific). USAspending contractors (representative): Leidos ($9.72M + 5-yr IDIQ ~$105.5M for NHRC), Parsons ($8.27M, NAMRU-6), A.C. Investment ($4.27M, NAMRU Cambodia), GDIT (~$4.13M), Westat (~$3.74M).

### 2.4 USAMRIID collaborators / licensees
Mapp Biopharmaceutical (ZMapp license 2009; separate $25.9M HHS/BARDA contract); Public Health Agency of Canada NML (other two ZMapp mAbs); SIGA + NIAID (TPOXX); DynPort Vaccine Co. (rF1-V plague).

### 2.5 CDMRP / TBDRP awardees (representative)
FY24 TBDRP: Microbiotix, UCLA, University of Maryland (each ≤$1.325M). FY22 TBDRP: Tufts, Virginia Tech, Columbia, Johns Hopkins.

### 2.6 GEIS funding recipients
Six DoD overseas labs (WRAIR-AFRIMS, NAMRU EURAFCENT, NAMRU INDO PACIFIC, NAMRU SOUTH, WRAIR-Africa/Nairobi, WRAIR Europe & Middle East/Tbilisi); broader network incl. NMRC, NHRC, Uniformed Services University, Defense Centers for Public Health.
