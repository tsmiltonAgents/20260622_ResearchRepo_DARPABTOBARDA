# DHS — Department of Homeland Security (Biodefense & Biosurveillance)

## Introduction
DHS (since 2003) is a principal civilian funder/operator of biodefense and biosurveillance: operating national labs (NBACC), urban aerosol-detection (BioWatch), cross-agency surveillance integration (NBIC), and funding R&D in-house (S&T) and via university Centers of Excellence and industry. The enterprise is in flux: the **FY2026 President's Budget proposes to dissolve the CWMD Office entirely.** Figures flagged inline; nothing classified asserted. ## Section 1 — Programmes and Mechanisms

### 1.1 S&T Directorate — Chemical-Biological Defense R&D
No division literally "chem-bio"; the portfolio is the **Chemical, Biological, and Explosive (CBE) Defense thrust**. S&T runs labs via the Office of National Laboratories. Signature programme **PANTHR** (~$34–40M/yr, FY21–24) provides CBRN(E) hazard/threat characterization; NBACC and CSAC are performers. **CSAC** (Chemical Security Analysis Center, est. 2006, Aberdeen MD). Scale: S&T R&D account FY2024 enacted $310.8M; the **CBE Defense line** is small ($32.6M FY22 → $13.8M FY24 → $11.3M FY26 request). NBACC/CSAC have no standalone appropriation (bundled in a ~$128M FY24 5-lab "Laboratory Facilities" PPA).

### 1.2 Countering Weapons of Mass Destruction Office (CWMD)
Leads DHS CBRN efforts. Formed administratively Dec 2017, established by statute Dec 2018 (P.L. 115-387, merging DNDO + most of the Office of Health Affairs) — with a **5-year sunset that expired Dec 21, 2023** (authorization lapsed; runs on annual appropriations). ~$409–452M/yr, ~240–260 staff. Houses BioWatch, NBIC, and nuclear/radiological programmes. **FY2026 budget proposes dissolving CWMD entirely** (BioWatch/Securing the Cities → CISA; NBIC → DHS Office of Health Security; rad-nuc → CBP; CBRN R&D → Coast Guard).

### 1.3 NBACC (National Biodefense Analysis and Countermeasures Center)
DHS's first national lab (est. 2004), Fort Detrick MD, BSL-4 capable. Provides biological-threat characterization (NBTCC, S&T-directed) and bioforensic attribution (NBFAC, FBI-directed). Operated as an FFRDC by **Battelle National Biodefense Institute (BNBI)**. FY2018 budget proposed closing it; restored ~$44.3M.

### 1.4 BioWatch and BD21
**BioWatch** (deployed 2003 post-anthrax) — aerosolized bio-agent detection in 30+ cities (~600 collectors); Gen-2 remains operational (~36 hr to result). Gen-3 (autonomous) cancelled April 2014 after cost ballooned to ~$5.8B (~$200M spent). ~$80M/yr; >$1B cumulative. Heavily criticized by GAO (GAO-16-99) and a 2012 LA Times investigation (false positives). **BD21 (Biological Detection for the 21st Century)** — intended BioWatch replacement (sensors + ML anomaly detection); tech demo 2018, acquisition 2019 (Level-1, ≥$1B); **paused June 2022, discontinued Sept 30, 2024** (immature tech). BioWatch Gen-2 remains the program of record.

### 1.5 NBIC (National Biosurveillance Integration Center)
Integrates human/animal/plant/food/environmental surveillance data. Created by the 2007 9/11 Commission Act (6 U.S.C. 195b); operational by Sept 30, 2008. ~$10–13M/yr. GAO-15-793 found partners found products of little use; GAO-24-106142 found improvement (COVID products).

### 1.6 Plum Island (PIADC) → NBAF (transferred to USDA)
PIADC (1954, USDA; DHS operation 2002–2003) — only U.S. facility for live FMD virus; winding down. **NBAF** (Manhattan KS, ~$1.25B, ribbon-cutting May 24, 2023) — first U.S. BSL-4 for large livestock. **Ownership transferred DHS→USDA** (MOA June 20, 2019; USDA owns/operates).

## Section 2 — Grantees and Awardees

### 2.1 Battelle / BNBI — marquee operating contract
**NBACC operating (FFRDC M&O) contract:** Sept 2015, **>$480M** (10-yr, contract HSHQDC-15-C-00064; ~$358–469M obligated across task orders). Predecessor HSHQDC-07-C-00020 (~$328.6M). [CONFIRMED]

### 2.2 BioWatch / BD21 vendors
Detector developers: **Northrop Grumman** (prime, Gen-2/Gen-3; 2010 task order $8.4M/up to $37M; team incl. Luminex, Research International); **LLNL + LANL** (BASIS origin, PCR assays). BioWatch lab-staffing/SETA contracts (USAspending): PAE $82.8M, HW Consulting $78.1M, Astrix $69.6M, A-TEK $54.4M+$47.3M, Amentum $45.5M, Tauri Group $30.4M, Signature Science $13.5M. **BD21: no prime/production contractor ever selected** (closed out Sept 30, 2024); technical advisors JPEO-CBRND, Army DEVCOM CBC, JHU APL, MIT Lincoln Lab. *(Smiths Detection $291M Jan 2019 IDIQ = radiation portal monitors, NOT biodetection.)*

### 2.3 University Centers of Excellence (now Emeritus)
- **FAZD** (Foreign Animal and Zoonotic Disease Defense, Texas A&M) — founded 2004, $18M/3yr; → rebranded **ZADD** (~2017, with Kansas State).
- **Food Protection and Defense Institute (FPDI, formerly NCFPD, U Minnesota)** — 2004, $15M/3yr.
- **CEEZAD** (Emerging and Zoonotic Animal Diseases, Kansas State) — 2010, $12M/6yr.
- Adjacent: PACER (Johns Hopkins), CREATE (USC, first DHS COE 2003), START (U Maryland).

### 2.4 Other performers
McCarthy Mortenson JV (built NBAF, ~$1.25B all-in); Zeteo Tech (Dec 2023 SBIR Phase III, $1.87M/up to $5.86M, digitalMALDI); WSP USA (NBACC design).
