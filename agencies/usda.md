# USDA — Department of Agriculture (Animal Health, Zoonotic Disease, Food-Supply Protection)

## Introduction
USDA runs the federal government's core programmes for livestock/poultry health, zoonotic-disease detection/containment, and food-supply security — across **APHIS**, **ARS**, and **NIFA**, coordinated with HHS (CDC/FDA) under One Health. Recipient/dollar figures below are **representative, not exhaustive**; uncertain ones flagged.

---

## Section 1 — Programmes

### (a) APHIS Veterinary Services & National Animal Health Laboratory Network (NAHLN)
APHIS Veterinary Services leads federal animal-disease surveillance/response. **NAHLN** (cooperative of APHIS, NIFA, AAVLD) has grown from 12 labs (2002) to ~**60 state/university labs**. FY2025: **$6M** noncompetitive operational funding; under the 2018 Farm Bill APHIS invested ~**$22M / 104 projects** ($5.1M FY21, $4.3M FY22, $2.29M FY23, ~$1M FY24).

### (b) National Bio and Agro-Defense Facility (NBAF), Manhattan KS
~520,000 sq ft high-containment lab; first U.S. facility with **large-animal BSL-4**. Replaces the 65-year-old BSL-3 Plum Island. Built by DHS S&T (~$1.25B, varies by source); **ownership transferred DHS→USDA spring 2023**; operated by ARS + APHIS; studies foot-and-mouth disease, African swine fever.

### (c) Agricultural Research Service (ARS) / National Animal Disease Center (NADC)
ARS = USDA's in-house research agency. Flagship animal-health site: **NADC, Ames, Iowa**, within the National Centers for Animal Health (co-located with APHIS NVSL and Center for Veterinary Biologics; 700+ staff).

### (d) NIFA competitive grants (AFRI)
**Agriculture and Food Research Initiative** — largest federal competitive ag-research program (2008 Farm Bill; reauthorized 2018 up to $700M/yr; ~$445.2M in FY2024). Funds Animal Health and Food Safety incl. antimicrobial resistance. Awards: $14.3M / 39 animal-health projects (FY2016); ~$2.6M (2024, AMR across the food chain); 2015 food-safety round ~$19M / 36 grantees (incl. $6.7M AMR).

### (e) Federal Select Agent Program (shared with CDC)
USDA's APHIS Division of Agricultural Select Agents and Toxins (DASAT) + CDC; regulates 63 select agents/toxins across ~230 registered entities (2024). Authority: Agricultural Bioterrorism Protection Act 2002.

### (f) Highly pathogenic avian influenza (H5N1) response
Since 2022 (poultry); from 2024 into dairy cattle. By late 2024 response costs >$1.4B (incl. ~$1.25B indemnity to poultry producers). Dec 2024 rule conditions indemnity on biosecurity audits. **Feb 26, 2025: Secretary Rollins announced up to $1B** — ~$500M biosecurity, ~$400M farmer relief/repopulation, **$100M vaccine/therapeutic research** (the "Poultry Innovation Grand Challenge"). Dairy: up to $1,500/farm biosecurity planning, $100/milk sample, 90% milk-loss compensation. April 2025: **Zoetis** got a conditional license for an H5N1 poultry vaccine.

### (g) One Health coordination
H5N1 and AMR work framed across APHIS, FSIS, NIFA, ARS with CDC and FDA (multisectoral surveillance across wild birds, poultry, mammals, people).

## Section 2 — Grantees / Recipients (representative)
- **Land-grant universities & NAHLN member labs** — ~60 veterinary diagnostic labs share the ~$6M annual operational pool + Farm Bill project grants ($22M / 104 projects).
- **NIFA AFRI grantees** — predominantly land-grant/research universities (e.g. University of Minnesota, turkey antibiotic reduction); 2015 round 36 grantees / $19M; FY2016 39 animal-health projects / $14.3M.
- **Animal-vaccine/diagnostic companies** — **Zoetis** (April 2025 H5N1 poultry vaccine conditional license); earlier stockpile poultry-vaccine work involved Merck and Ceva. (Boehringer Ingelheim role unconfirmed.) The $100M HPAI vaccine/therapeutics channel is open to companies and universities (awardees not yet enumerated).
