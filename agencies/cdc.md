# CDC — Centers for Disease Control and Prevention

## Introduction
CDC anchors the **public-health, surveillance, and laboratory-capacity** side of U.S. pandemic preparedness (vs. DARPA buying breakthroughs and BARDA buying countermeasures). It funds the standing detection-and-response infrastructure: state/local/territorial health departments, laboratory networks, surveillance systems, modeling, and global disease-detection partnerships. **Most CDC "funding" flows through cooperative agreements** (grant-like) to jurisdictions and academic partners. This covers programmes thoroughly and gives **representative/major** grantees. Two scope caveats: the **Strategic National Stockpile moved to ASPR in 2018** (no longer CDC); and CDC saw **major proposed budget cuts/disruption in 2025** (figures flagged/dated).

## Section 1 — Programmes and mechanisms

### 1.1 Epidemiology and Laboratory Capacity (ELC) Cooperative Agreement
CDC's flagship domestic infectious-disease funding strategy. Start **1995** (~30 yrs). Administered by NCEZID. Current 5-yr agreement **CK24-0002**: **65 jurisdictions** (50 states + 7 large local + 8 territorial). Core funding ≈**$364M** (2024, snippet-sourced). **Primary conduit for COVID supplements** — "$19B+" to 64 jurisdictions (a cumulative "$42B" also appears; CRRSA $19.11B tranche verified).

### 1.2 Public Health Emergency Preparedness (PHEP) Cooperative Agreement
All-hazards readiness for health departments. Start **2002** (post-anthrax/9-11). Office of Readiness and Response. **62 awardees** (50 states + 4 localities: Chicago, LA County, NYC, DC + 8 territories). **$735M** appropriated FY2023–FY2024. FY2026 budget *proposed* cutting to $350M.

### 1.3 Center for Forecasting and Outbreak Analytics (CFA)
"National Weather Service for infectious diseases." Launched Apr 2022; established as a center Jan 2023. Founding **$200M from American Rescue Plan**. First investment (Oct 2021): **$26M total, $21M to five academic institutions** (Albany/SUNY, Johns Hopkins, Stanford, Utah, Harvard Chan). By Dec 2025, Dr. Jason Asher listed as director (Dylan George departed).

### 1.4 Influenza Division & surveillance
WHO Collaborating Center since **1956** (one of seven worldwide); produces candidate vaccine viruses for WHO vaccine-composition recommendations. **ILINet** (~4,000 outpatient providers); virologic surveillance (~400 labs); **FluView**; **Influenza Risk Assessment Tool (IRAT)** scoring pandemic risk of animal flu viruses (e.g. H5N1, H7N9).

### 1.5 Laboratory Response Network (LRN)
National/international threat-response lab network. Start **1999** (CDC + FBI + APHL). **LRN-B** (~120 biological labs, all 50 states) and **LRN-C** (53 chemical labs).

### 1.6 Advanced Molecular Detection (AMD)
NGS + bioinformatics + epidemiology. Start **2014**. Appropriation "now $40M/year" (was ~$30M). Funds the field partly via ELC.

### 1.7 BioSense / National Syndromic Surveillance Program (NSSP)
Near-real-time syndromic surveillance (chiefly ED-visit data). BioSense launched **2003** (Bioterrorism Act 2002); → BioSense 2.0 (~2010–11) → NSSP (BioSense now the platform; ESSENCE is the analytic tool). >7,500 facilities, ~85% of U.S. EDs, >10.2M messages/day.

### 1.8 Strategic National Stockpile (SNS) — historical (now ASPR)
Began as National Pharmaceutical Stockpile (1998/1999, ~$51M); renamed SNS Mar 1, 2003; **transferred CDC→ASPR Oct 1, 2018** (enabling authority PAHPRA 2013, not the 2019 PAHPAIA). Now $7–8B+ inventory under ASPR.

### 1.9 Federal Select Agent Program (FSAP) — regulatory, not a funder
Regulates possession/use/transfer of select agents/toxins. **Jointly CDC + USDA APHIS** (Bioterrorism Act 2002, 42 CFR Part 73). ~230 registered entities (2024). Purely regulatory.

### 1.10 Emerging Infections Programs (EIP)
Active population-based surveillance + applied research. Founded **~1995**. CDC now states **12 sites** (core 10 ABCs states). Components: ABCs, FoodNet (with USDA/FDA), HAIC.

### 1.11 Global Health Security Agenda (GHSA)
Launched **Feb 2014** (26 nations + WHO/FAO/OIE). CDC led the Joint External Evaluation (JEE) tool; works with 17 Phase I partner countries; operates 60+ overseas offices. >70 countries signed; framing now "GHSA 2028."

## Section 2 — Grantees and awardees (representative)

### 2.1 ELC / PHEP recipients
Formula-style cooperative agreements covering essentially the entire U.S. public-health system: **ELC 65 recipients** (50 states + 7 large local + 8 territorial); **PHEP 62 recipients** (50 states + Chicago/LA County/NYC/DC + 8 territories). Notable: the 4 directly-funded PHEP localities.

### 2.2 Emerging Infections Programs — academic partners (core 10 ABCs)
UC Berkeley (CA); U Colorado HSC; Yale (CT); Emory (GA); Johns Hopkins (MD); APIC (MN); U New Mexico HSC; U Rochester (NY); OHSU (OR); Vanderbilt (TN). ~$3.6M average/site (2014).

### 2.3 CFA early academic awards (Oct 2021, $26M total / $21M academic)
Albany/SUNY, Johns Hopkins, Stanford, Utah, Harvard Chan.

### 2.4 Insight Net (Outbreak Analytics & Disease Modeling Network) — 13 core awardees
Announced Sept 2023: **up to $262.5M / 5 yrs**; **>$148M awarded to date** (ceiling vs obligated). Hubs: Carnegie Mellon (Delphi), Clemson (DMA-PRIME), Emory (CIDMATH ~$17.5M), Johns Hopkins (CORI), Kaiser Permanente SoCal (C-CORE), Northeastern (Epistorm), UC San Diego (Resilient Shield), U Michigan (MICOM ~$17.5M), U Minnesota (MADMC), UNC Chapel Hill (ACCIDDA), UT Austin (epiENGAGE ~$27.5M), U Utah (ForeSITE), International Responder System (SOAR). *(Harvard/Boston Children's are NOT core Insight Net awardees.)*

### 2.5 COVID-19 Forecast Hub (predecessor)
Reich Lab, UMass Amherst (launched Mar/Apr 2020) — direct predecessor to CFA forecasting.

## Key uncertainties
ELC COVID total ($19B+ vs $42B cumulative); ELC core $364M (snippet); PHEP $735M (FY26 proposed cut to $350M); Insight Net ceiling vs obligated; EIP 12 vs core-10; AMD ~$40M; FSAP ~230 (regulatory); SNS authority = PAHPRA 2013; CFA leadership 2025 medium-confidence.
