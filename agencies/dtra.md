# DTRA — Defense Threat Reduction Agency

## Introduction
DTRA is DoD's lead agency for countering WMD (CBRNE). Established Oct 1, 1998; HQ Fort Belvoir, VA; reports to USD(A&S); a Combat Support Agency. In the biodefense ecosystem it is the **S&T base manager for the DoD Chemical and Biological Defense Program (CBDP)** and home of **Cooperative Threat Reduction (CTR)**. It is the DoD analogue to DARPA BTO — both fund threat-agnostic, platform-oriented biodefense science via BAAs — but DTRA sustains S&T and transitions matured tech toward fielding (often via government labs like USAMRIID). Program-level budgets are largely not broken out publicly and may be partly classified; awardee lists are **representative**.

## Section 1 — Programmes and Mechanisms

### 1.1 R&D Directorate / Chemical & Biological Technologies Dept (RD-CB / "J9CB")
Leads DoD's primary CBRN S&T program (450+ PhDs; ~>$1B R&D budget, approx/undated). **RD-CB serves as the Joint Science and Technology Office (JSTO) for the CBDP.** Bio thrusts: Detection & Diagnostics; Protection & Hazard Mitigation; Vaccines & Therapeutics — with emphasis on **Biologics On Demand**, Automated Threat Detection, Alternative Testing Models (organ-on-chip), and AI/ML.

### 1.2 JSTO-CBD and the CBDP
JSTO manages the S&T component of the CBDP; PMs are "Science and Technology Managers." CBDP splits into **RDT&E** (S&T + advanced dev, executed by DTRA/JSTO, ~TRL 1–6) and **Procurement** (executed by **JPEO-CBRND**). CBDP scale: FY2024 request ~$1.79B / actual $1.479B; FY2025 request $1.657B / enacted $1.380B; FY2026 request ~$1.41B. "~$1.7B CBDP" is a *request* figure; no clean public "JSTO budget" line.

### 1.3 Funding mechanisms — BAAs (HDTRA1 prefix), OTAs, SBIR/STTR
Awards carry the **HDTRA1** prefix. Flagship: **HDTRA1-25-S-0001 "Fundamental Research to Counter WMD"** (open Oct 2024–Sep 2034, TRL 1–4, ~$500M/10yr ceiling, ~10 grants; ≥30% university contribution preferred). Plus the RD-CB/JSTO Chemical & Biological Technologies Fundamental Research BAA series. Consortium OTAs: **MTEC** and **MCDC**. SBIR/STTR: Phase I $150–250k, Phase II ~$1.0–1.7M.

### 1.4 Cooperative Threat Reduction (CTR) / Biological Threat Reduction Program (BTRP, formerly CBEP)
Created by the Nunn-Lugar Act 1991. Bio mission emerged early (Soviet Biopreparat). Naming: **BWPP (1998) → CBEP (~2009–2017) → BTRP (current)**. Purpose: enable partner countries to prevent/detect/prepare for outbreaks (biosurveillance, biosafety/biosecurity, cooperative research). Geography expanded beyond former-Soviet states (FY2008 NDAA removed limits; ~30+ countries). Scale: DoD invested ~$7B in CTR overall in first ~15 yrs; bio-specific >$430M cumulative FY1998–2007. BTRP/CBEP: FY2023 ~$235M → FY2024 ~$218M → FY2025 ~$160M → FY2026 request ~$139M (steep decline). *Disinformation note: 2022 Russian "Ukraine bioweapons lab" claims were debunked; a Russian UNSC resolution was defeated Nov 2, 2022.*

### 1.5 Threat-agnostic countermeasures & DOMANE
"Shield and Spear" pathogen-agnostic strategy: **SPADES** (sequence-based detection), **RAPTER** (expedited MCM response), **ONTARGET** (solicited Jun 2025). **DOMANE (Discovery of Medical Countermeasures Against Novel Entities)**, est. late 2019: ML/HTS to repurpose ~7,500 FDA-approved drugs; for COVID advanced remdesivir, famotidine, celecoxib, ivermectin (trial partners Quantum Leap, Leidos, Brooke Army Medical Center). No public DOMANE dollar figure.

### 1.6 Biosurveillance — Biosurveillance Ecosystem (BSVE)
Cloud-based analytics aggregating WHO/FAO/OIE/ProMED + open-source feeds; "disease-agnostic," One Health. Funded by JSTO-CBD, later co-developed with DHS NBIC. Prime: **Digital Infuzion**; Draper (COMBS prototype), MIT Lincoln Lab (requirements), LANL (Epi Archive/EpiGrid). 2019 self-reported: 170+ sources, 700+ users, 43M+ calculations/day. Status uncertain (apparently transitioned to DHS CWMD ~2021). *BSVE not documented detecting COVID-19; DTRA COVID work used EpiGrid (LANL) and PatchSim (Virginia Tech).*

## Section 2 — Representative Grantees and Awardees

### 2.1 Companies (FY2015 cohort + recent)
MRIGlobal ($14.77M, WGS diagnostic, Jan 2015); Digital Infuzion ($12.81M, BSVE, Apr 2015); 908 Devices ($11.53M, uGC mass-spec, Apr 2015); EcoHealth Alliance ($4.48M, GRITS, Apr 2015); GE Global Research ($3.12M, AST, Dec 2015); Battelle ($3.14M, AChE reactivators, Mar 2015); Kitware ($2.34M, BSVE Phase II); ENSCO ($0.84M); Gryphon Scientific ($1.20M two awards). **Recent:** Battelle **STEP 2.0** ($52.9M base / $93.3M total, Feb–Mar 2026, sole award); Ginkgo Bioworks (MCDC + ~$43.5M task order on Battelle-led DoD IAC HD-TAT); Leidos, Quantum Leap Healthcare (DOMANE trials).

### 2.2 Universities / institutions
University of Pennsylvania ($4.54M, AAV chemical-threat protector); Massachusetts General Hospital ($5.22M, Q-VaxCelerate Q-fever vaccine); Columbia ($2.69M, flu forecasting); Virginia Tech (PatchSim model). C-WMD BAA mandates/prefers ≥30% university participation.

### 2.3 U.S. national labs
LANL (Epi Archive, EpiGrid); MIT Lincoln Lab (BSVE requirements); Draper (COMBS). (Sandia/LLNL/PNNL/ORNL associated with DoD chem-bio S&T but no public DTRA bio award figures confirmed.)

### 2.4 Overseas partner labs (former CBEP/BTRP) — partner-government-owned
Richard Lugar Center, Tbilisi, Georgia (built via DTRA/Georgian MoD, operational 2013, Georgian-owned/funded since 2018); Central Reference Laboratory, Almaty, Kazakhstan (~$102M–$130M, transferred 2017); Ukraine (~$200M since 2005 supporting 46 Ukrainian-owned labs; surveillance/diagnostics only — no bioweapons); CRLs/zonal labs in Azerbaijan, Uzbekistan.

## Key uncertainties
No public budget breakouts for JSTO-as-a-unit, DOMANE, or specific MCM programs (aggregated in CBDP RDT&E, partly classified); BWPP→CBEP→BTRP rename years not cleanly documented; overseas-lab cost figures conflict; BSVE metrics vendor-self-reported, status unresolved; awardee tables skew to FY2015 (richest clean dataset) + recent press.
