# DOE National Laboratories & NNSA (Biosurveillance, Biosecurity, Infectious-Disease Science)

## Introduction
DOE/NNSA are not biomedical agencies; their biological footprint grows from (1) the Office of Science's **Biological and Environmental Research (BER)** program (genomics/microbial systems biology for *energy and environment*) and (2) **NNSA's national-security mission** (pathogen-detection tech, biodefense R&D at the weapons labs). During COVID-19, DOE linked these via the **National Virtual Biotechnology Laboratory (NVBL)**. **Structural fact:** DOE funds its own 17 national labs (FFRDCs, government-owned/contractor-operated) — so "awardees" are largely the M&O *operators*. Two corrections: the NVBL CARES Act figure is **$99.5M** (the "~$30M" premise unverified); the **IBIS/PCR-ESI-MS platform is Ibis Biosciences/Abbott, not an LLNL invention.**

## Section 1 — Programmes and Capabilities

### 1.1 BER & the Joint Genome Institute (JGI)
BER's Genomic Science Program funds systems biology of **plants/microbes** for bioenergy, carbon cycling, biogeochemistry — **non-human scope** (no human pathogens/biosurveillance). ~$900M enacted FY2024 (FY2026 request proposed a ~56% cut to ~$395M). **JGI** (BER user facility since 2004, at Lawrence Berkeley NL): high-throughput sequencing for energy/environment — not clinical pathogen surveillance.

### 1.2 NNSA Defense Nuclear Nonproliferation (DNN) & the chem-bio lineage
Present-day DNN is **nuclear/radiological-only** (no chem-bio line). The chem-bio link is **historical/lab-based**: BioWatch "is heavily based on BASIS, developed within the **Chemical and Biological National Security Program of the NNSA** by scientists at **Lawrence Livermore and Los Alamos**" (BASIS → DHS BioWatch, 2003; LLNL follow-on APDS). *Corrections: international pathogen-security programs are State (BEP)/DoD (CBEP), not NNSA; the 2024 LLNL biodefense supercomputer was DoD-funded ($20M).*

### 1.3 LLNL — biodetection & biosecurity
**Lawrence Livermore Microbial Detection Array (LLMDA):** microarray identifying any sequenced microbe (<24 hr); licensed 2012 to MOgene and Statens Serum Institut. Runs the **Biodefense Knowledge Center** and the **only BSL-3 among DOE labs** (online Jan 2008); applied bio work often "work for others" for DHS. COVID: ML antibody screening (Faissol/Desautels), predictive spike structures (Zemla); part of NVBL.

### 1.4 LANL — bioinformatics & epidemiology
Hosts the **HIV Sequence Database** (since 1987) + hepatitis C/Ebola/influenza databases. **COVID-19 Viral Genome Analysis Pipeline (cov.lanl.gov)** using GISAID. **Bette Korber** was corresponding author of the D614G spike-infectivity paper (*Cell*, July 2020). **EpiCast** agent-based epidemic model (Census-based synthetic populations).

### 1.5 Sandia — diagnostics & global biosecurity
**SpinDx** centrifugal-microfluidic rapid diagnostics (<15 min; a biothreat instrument got ~$4M from NIAID). **Global Chemical and Biological Security (GCBS)** (since 2003): partnerships in 145+ countries, 19,000+ trained. COVID: medical-resource modeling, PPE testing.

### 1.6 PNNL & ORNL
**PNNL** — Chemical & Biological Signatures Science (threat-agent signatures; biosurveillance analytics). **ORNL** — Summit supercomputer analysis proposing the COVID "bradykinin storm" (Jacobson; *eLife*); later 3CLpro/NEMO work.

### 1.7 National Virtual Biotechnology Laboratory (NVBL)
Created Apr 16, 2020, led via the DOE Office of Science; coordinated across all **17 DOE labs** (molecular design, testing, modeling, viral fate/transport, materials/manufacturing). Funded by CARES Act — authoritative figure **$99.5M** to Office of Science labs (the "~$30M" premise unverified).

## Section 2 — Grantees / Awardees (Operators and Partners)
DOE funds its own FFRDCs (GOCO model). Principal "awardees" = the **M&O contractors**:

| Lab | Operating entity (M&O) | Sponsor | Members |
|---|---|---|---|
| LLNL | Lawrence Livermore National Security, LLC | NNSA (DE-AC52-07NA27344) | Bechtel, Univ. of California, BWXT, Amentum |
| LANL | Triad National Security, LLC | NNSA (89233218CNA000001) | Battelle, Texas A&M System, Univ. of California |
| Sandia | NTESS, LLC | NNSA (DE-NA-0003525) | Honeywell subsidiary |
| LBNL (JGI) | University of California | Office of Science | UC since 1943 |
| PNNL | Battelle Memorial Institute | Office of Science | Battelle since 1965 |
| ORNL | UT-Battelle, LLC | Office of Science | Univ. of Tennessee + Battelle |

**University/private partners** enter via the operating LLCs (UC, Texas A&M, U Tennessee) and via **CRADAs** (partner brings own funds; DOE labs contributed >$7M to university CRADAs FY14–18). **External funding into this work:** LLNL biodetection often DHS "work for others"; Sandia biothreat diagnostic ~$4M NIAID; 2024 LLNL biodefense supercomputer $20M DoD.

## Key uncertainties
NVBL "$30M" corrected to $99.5M; IBIS = Ibis Biosciences/Abbott (not LLNL); FY2025-enacted/FY2026-request BER & NNSA figures are requests/CR-level; some Sandia/PNNL metrics from search summaries; present-day NNSA DNN is nuclear-only (chem-bio is legacy + lab "work for others"); classified NNSA detection specifics not asserted.
