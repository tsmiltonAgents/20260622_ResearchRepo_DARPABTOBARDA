# NIH / NIAID as a Funder of Pandemic Preparedness, Infectious-Disease, Biosecurity & Medical-Countermeasure R&D

## Introduction

The U.S. **National Institutes of Health (NIH)**, and within it the **National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases (NIAID)**, is the federal government's principal funder of *upstream* research underpinning pandemic preparedness and medical countermeasures (MCMs). Where **BARDA** (within HHS/ASPR) funds advanced development, manufacturing and procurement of MCMs, and **DARPA's Biological Technologies Office (BTO)** funds high-risk defense-oriented biotechnology, NIAID occupies the basic-to-translational research space: pathogen biology, immunogen design, antiviral discovery, clinical trial networks, and a national high-containment laboratory infrastructure. NIAID is also HHS/NIH's formally designated lead institute for civilian biodefense and CBRN medical countermeasures.

This document covers (1) the major relevant programmes and funding mechanisms, and (2) representative grantees/awardees. **The grantee list is representative, not exhaustive** — NIH makes tens of thousands of awards annually. A methodological note: `nih.gov` and `niaid.nih.gov` block automated fetching, so figures were corroborated via NIH search snippets, institutional press releases, and NIH-hosted PubMed Central articles; the `.gov` URLs cited are valid for human browsing.

## Section 1 — Programmes / Funding Mechanisms

### 1.1 Vaccine Research Center (VRC) — intramural
Established by Presidential Executive Order in **1997** (opened **2000**), the Dale and Betty Bumpers VRC is NIAID's intramural engine for structure-based vaccine and biologics design (HIV, coronaviruses, influenza, malaria). It pioneered the **prefusion-stabilization** immunogen-design approach (the "2P"/S-2P spike mutations) that enabled the rapid COVID-19 mRNA vaccine. Intramurally funded — *no authoritative standalone budget figure found.*

### 1.2 Vaccine and Treatment Evaluation Units (VTEUs)
Clinical-trial site network established **1962**, now the site arm of the Infectious Diseases Clinical Research Consortium (IDCRC). The **2020 solicitation (RFA-AI-20-021)** stated intent of **~$29M/year for seven years** — *RFA-stated intent.*

### 1.3 Centers of Excellence for Influenza Research and Response (CEIRR) — formerly CEIRS
**CEIRS** established **2007**; contracts concluded **March 31, 2021**. **CEIRR** (renamed "…Response") launched **2021**: seven-year contracts to **five institutions**, **first-year funding ~$24M**. Studies influenza natural history, transmission, pathogenesis, and surveillance.

### 1.4 Collaborative Influenza Vaccine Innovation Centers (CIVICs)
Launched **2019** to develop durable, broadly protective ("universal") influenza vaccines. NIAID provided **up to ~$51M first-year funding** over **seven years**. Structure: 3 Vaccine Centers, 2 Clinical Cores, 1 Manufacturing/Tox Core, 1 Coordination Center. *(A "$200M+" figure refers to the University of Maryland CIVICs Clinical Core contract, not the whole program.)*

### 1.5 Centers for Research in Emerging Infectious Diseases (CREID)
Established **August 2020**, a global network investigating how/where pathogens spill over from wildlife to humans. NIAID awarded **11 grants, total first-year value ~$17M**, intends **~$82M over 5 years**. **Coordinating Center: RTI International** (with Duke). ~10 named research centers across 30+ countries.

### 1.6 Antiviral Drug Discovery (AViDD) Centers for Pathogens of Pandemic Concern
Announced **May 18, 2022**: **~$577M to establish nine AViDD Centers** (U19 cooperative agreements, RFA-AI-21-050), under the broader **Antiviral Program for Pandemics (APP)**. Initial period 3 years (extendable to 5). Goal: direct-acting antivirals (esp. oral/outpatient COVID-19) against pandemic-threat virus families. (Center leads in §2.)

### 1.7 NIAID Pandemic Preparedness Plan & "Prototype Pathogen" Approach
NIAID published the **"Pandemic Preparedness Plan"** in **December 2021**: two prongs — **priority pathogens** (influenza, SARS-CoV-2, Ebola) and **prototype pathogens** (one representative virus per pandemic-potential family). *Scope flag:* NIAID's Nov 2021 workshop selected prototype pathogens from **10 viral families** (or **9** when the five Bunyavirales families are grouped) — not "25+."

### 1.8 ReVAMPP — Vaccines and Monoclonal Antibodies for Pandemic Preparedness
Operationalizes the prototype-pathogen approach for vaccines/mAbs. Solicited via 2023 RFAs; **U19 center awards made in 2024**. NIAID expects **~$100M/year**. Structure: **seven research centers + a Coordination and Data Sharing Center (RTI International**, PI Greg Sempowski).

### 1.9 ACTIV & ACTT (COVID-19 therapeutics)
- **ACTIV** (Accelerating COVID-19 Therapeutic Interventions and Vaccines), launched **April 2020**, an NIH public-private partnership convened by FNIH; ran master-protocol trials ACTIV-1 through ACTIV-6.
- **ACTT** (Adaptive COVID-19 Treatment Trial), NIAID-sponsored, began **Feb 21, 2020** — first U.S. trial of a COVID-19 therapeutic. ACTT-1 (1,063 patients) showed remdesivir shortened recovery 15→11 days.

### 1.10 RADx (Rapid Acceleration of Diagnostics)
Funded by a **$1.5B** congressional supplement (Apr 24, 2020), administered largely through **NIBIB**. Sub-programs: RADx Tech, RADx-ATP, RADx-rad, RADx-UP. *(NIH-wide/NIBIB-led, not NIAID-specific.)*

### 1.11 Moderna mRNA-1273 / NIAID VRC Co-Development
mRNA-1273 was **co-developed by Moderna and the NIAID VRC** after the Jan 2020 sequence release. Key NIAID scientists: Barney Graham (prefusion-stabilization), Kizzmekia Corbett; spike structure with University of Texas at Austin.

### 1.12 Radiation and Nuclear Countermeasures Program (RNCP)
Launched **2005** within NIAID (post-Project BioShield). Develops MCMs and biodosimetry for radiological/nuclear emergencies. Historic scale ~**$45–50M/year** (c. 2011 — dated). Research arm: **Centers for Medical Countermeasures against Radiation (CMCR)** (est. 2005; currently 3 centers).

### 1.13 Rocky Mountain Laboratories (RML) — intramural high-containment
NIAID's intramural campus in Hamilton, Montana, with BSL-2/3/**4** space. Central to the **VSV-vectored Ebola vaccine (rVSV-ZEBOV, behind Ervebo)**; produced first U.S. EM images of SARS-CoV-2 (Feb 2020); established the Syrian hamster COVID-19 model.

### 1.14 Biosecurity / Biodefense Infrastructure and DURC
- Post-2001: NIAID built ~50 biodefense initiatives organized around **Category A/B/C priority pathogens**.
- **Biocontainment network:** ~**$1B** to build **12 Regional Biocontainment Labs (BSL-3)** and **2 National Biocontainment Labs (BSL-4)** — the **NEIDL at Boston University** and the **Galveston National Laboratory at UTMB**.
- **DURC / Gain-of-function:** Oct 17, 2014 funding pause; **HHS P3CO Framework (Dec 19, 2017)** replaced it with case-by-case ePPP review. (2024 consolidated DURC/PEPP policy indicates ongoing flux.)
