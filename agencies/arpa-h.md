# ARPA-H: Programmes and Awardees Relevant to Pandemic Preparedness, Infectious Disease, Vaccines, Diagnostics, and Rapid Medical-Countermeasure Response

## Scope and caveat

The **Advanced Research Projects Agency for Health (ARPA-H)** was established in 2022 within the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) and is modeled on DARPA: tenure-limited program managers with high autonomy, milestone-based contracts (frequently Other Transaction Agreements), and a "high-risk, high-reward" mandate to deliver health breakthroughs "in years, not decades." Its budget has been roughly **$1.5 billion per year** since FY2023.

**This document is deliberately scoped to the subset of ARPA-H's portfolio relevant to pandemic preparedness, infectious disease, vaccines, diagnostics, and rapid medical-countermeasure (MCM) response. ARPA-H is a broad health agency, and much — arguably most — of its portfolio is unrelated to infectious disease.** Its largest and most numerous programmes address cancer, regenerative medicine, organ/tissue repair, cardiovascular and metabolic disease, aging/healthspan, mental health, rural care delivery, and health-system cybersecurity. There is **no single flagship ARPA-H "pandemic," "biosurveillance," or "infectious disease" mission programme**; the relevant work is distributed across vaccine-design, antimicrobial-resistance, biomanufacturing, airborne-pathogen-detection, and biosensor programmes — several of which are dual-use (e.g., cancer detection platforms that also target infection).

A structural note that affects precision throughout: ARPA-H's public site does **not** map every programme to a mission office, and its individual award/awardee detail pages are JavaScript-rendered, which blocks automated retrieval and means many **per-awardee dollar splits are not publicly disclosed** — only programme-level ceilings and selected press-release figures are firm. Nearly all ARPA-H award figures are "**up to**" ceilings (milestone-gated contracts), **not guaranteed disbursements**. Uncertain or conflicting figures are flagged inline as **[FLAG]**.

## Section 1 — Programmes

### 1.1 Mission-office structure

ARPA-H organizes its work into **four mission offices** (source: https://arpa-h.gov/about/our-research):

1. **Health Science Futures** — "Expanding what's technically possible." Accelerates advances across research areas and removes cross-cutting technical limitations (e.g., regulating biology to cure rather than treat disease, tissue/organ repair, AI biology platforms). Houses some manufacturing-platform work (e.g., cell-free viral-vector production).

2. **Proactive Health** — "Keeping people from being patients." Reduces the likelihood that people become patients, explicitly including threats that are "viral, bacterial, chemical, physical, or psychological" — i.e., infection prevention, prophylaxis, detection, and population-level prevention. This is the office most squarely aligned with the *prevention* side of infectious disease.

3. **Resilient Systems** — "Building integrated health care systems." Develops capabilities, business models, and integrations that let health systems "endure crises such as pandemics, social disruption, and economic instability," including supply chain, manufacturing, and logistics disruption mitigation. **Note:** despite the explicit pandemic framing, there is no separately *named* Resilient Systems programme dedicated to pandemic response or MCM supply chains; such work is funded through a rolling Innovative Solutions Opening (ISO) rather than a named programme **[FLAG — notable gap]**.

4. **Scalable Solutions** — "Reaching everyone quickly." Tackles geography, distribution, manufacturing, data, and economies of scale. This office houses most of ARPA-H's **distributed/on-demand biomanufacturing** work relevant to rapid MCM response.

### 1.2 ARPANET-H (nationwide health innovation network)

**ARPANET-H** is a nationwide health-innovation network (announced **September 26, 2023** **[FLAG — one third-party source, SSTI, dates it Sept 28]**), built on a DARPA-inspired hub-and-spoke model. It is anchored by **three regional hubs**, each on a five-year period of performance (one-year base + four one-year options):

| Hub | Location | Operator | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Customer Experience Hub** | Dallas, TX (Pegasus Park) | Advanced Technology International (ATI) | Ensuring solutions are accessible, needed, and adopted |
| **Investor Catalyst Hub** | Cambridge, MA (greater Boston) | VentureWell | Speeding transition of ideas into practical, accessible solutions |
| **Stakeholder & Operations Hub** | Washington, D.C. (Bowen Building) | ARPA-H directly | Coordinating federal partners; managing ARPA-H programmes |

A "$2.5 billion" figure for the network appears only in a third-party source (SSTI) and is **not confirmed on arpa-h.gov**; no per-hub dollar amounts were found **[FLAG]**.

### 1.3 Founding, leadership, and budget (context)

- **Established:** Funded via the Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2022 (P.L. 117-103, signed March 15, 2022); formally authorized via the PREVENT Pandemics Act within the Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2023 (P.L. 117-328). Housed within NIH but reporting to the HHS Secretary, off the NIH campus.
- **Leadership:** Renee Wegrzyn was the inaugural director (sworn in October 11, 2022; a presidential appointment, not Senate-confirmed). She was removed by the Trump administration on February 14, 2025; Jason Roos served as acting director; **Alicia Jackson** became director in late 2025 **[FLAG — swearing-in date conflicts: late Oct vs. Nov 2025]**.
- **Budget:** ~**$1.5 billion enacted** each year FY2023–FY2025 (FY23 P.L. 117-328; FY24 P.L. 118-47; FY25 full-year CR P.L. 119-4). FY2022 was a startup-year exception at **$1.0 billion**. **[FLAG]** The "$2.5 billion" sometimes cited for FY2024 was the President's *request*, **not** the enacted amount (Congress held funding flat at $1.5B); a widely circulated claim that ARPA-H "received $2.5B" for FY2024 is inaccurate. For FY2026, the Trump administration requested a cut to ~$945M, which Congress reportedly rejected, maintaining $1.5B **[FLAG — final public-law citation not located]**.

### 1.4 Programmes most relevant to infectious disease / vaccines / diagnostics / rapid response

#### Vaccines and broadly protective antigen design

**APECx — Antigens Predicted for Broad Viral Efficacy through Computational Experimentation**
- **Office / PM:** Computational vaccine design. PM listed as Shannon Greene, Ph.D. on the current page; the 2023 launch release listed Andy Kilianski, Ph.D. **[FLAG — likely a PM change]**.
- **Purpose:** Use computational/AI methods to design **broadly protective antigens against entire viral families** (a "one vaccine per family" proactive pandemic-prevention vision), rather than one virus at a time.
- **Years / scale:** Launched October 10, 2023; awards announced September 25, 2024. Programme total **up to $204 million**.
- This is the agency's most direct **universal/broadly protective vaccine** effort. It is vaccine *discovery/design*, not manufacturing or detection.

**CUREIT — mRNA platform (cancer + emerging infectious disease)**
- **Purpose:** A self-amplifying/multi-target mRNA platform with applications spanning anti-cancer and **anti-microbial/anti-infective** vaccines. Frequently described as ARPA-H's first grant.
- **Years / scale:** Announced August 28, 2023. **Up to $24 million [FLAG — Yale's release says "$25M over three years"]**.

#### Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) — therapeutics and diagnostics

ARPA-H has stated its AMR investment "tops $150M" across three programmes:

**DARTS — Defeating Antibiotic Resistance through Transformative Solutions**
- **Purpose:** Ultra-high-throughput single-cell imaging/culturing plus machine learning to detect resistant bacteria and match the right antibiotic in real time (a diagnostics + stewardship play).
- **Years / scale:** Announced ~September 26–27, 2023. **Up to $104 million.**

**PROTECT — Pro/Prebiotic Regulation for Optimized Treatment and Eradication of Clinical Threats**
- **Purpose:** Inhalable probiotic/prebiotic lung-microbiome formulations to prevent/treat infection and reduce antibiotic reliance (targets include *P. aeruginosa* and *S. aureus*).
- **Years / scale:** Announced October 10, 2024. **Up to $22.7 million total.**

**TARGET — Transforming Antibiotic R&D with Generative AI**
- **Purpose:** Generative-AI-driven discovery of novel antibiotics; goal of 12+ novel preclinical antibiotics plus an open-source database.
- **Years / scale:** Announced September 26, 2024. **Up to $27 million.**

#### Broad-spectrum antiviral / antibacterial therapeutics

**SCRIBE — Synthetic Carbohydrate Receptors for Infection Blocking**
- **Purpose:** Broad-spectrum antiviral nasal spray that binds viral glycans; reported active in models against SARS-CoV-2, Ebola, Nipah, Hendra, and Marburg.
- **Years / scale:** **Up to $12.7 million** (award start February 23, 2026).

**MIGHTY — precision bacteriophage / microbiome platform**
- **Purpose:** Precision bacteriophage tools to rebalance the microbiome (relevant to infection and AMR).
- **Years / scale:** **Up to $28 million** (start September 22, 2025).

**DOORs** — a pathogen–host mapping programme that is infectious-disease relevant but had **no publicly announced awardees** at the time of research **[FLAG — early stage]**.

#### Diagnostics, biosensors, and early detection

**BREATHE — Building Resilient Environments for Air and Total Health**
- **Purpose:** The programme most relevant to **airborne-pathogen detection and biosurveillance**: (1) indoor-air biosensors to rapidly detect airborne biothreats, (2) respiratory-risk-assessment software, (3) building-control interventions. Addresses transmission of flu, pneumonia, and emerging respiratory threats.
- **Years / scale:** Launched April 10, 2024; awardees kicked off ~September 29, 2025. Programme total **up to $156 million over 5 years**. **[FLAG — minor naming discrepancy: "Environment" vs "Environments" across ARPA-H pages.]**

**Delphi — wearable & ingestible biosensors for earliest disease detection**
- **Purpose:** Modular "chiplet" platform for wearable and ingestible biosensors that continuously monitor deep biological signals to detect the earliest signs of disease (a general early-detection/wearables programme, dual-use for infection onset).
- **Years / scale:** Announced March 10, 2026; solicitation open (full proposals due May 13, 2026), **no awardees yet**. Funding scale not stated.

**OCULAB — Ocular Laboratory for Analysis of Biomarkers**
- **Purpose:** Wearable tear-duct nanosensor for continuous biomarker monitoring with closed-loop dosing (a wearables/early-detection platform; initial target dry eye disease).
- **Years / scale:** Launched October 18, 2024. **[FLAG — "up to $75.8M over 4 years, 4 teams" appears in a search summary but is NOT confirmed on the official page.]**

**POSEIDON — Platform Optimizing SynBio for Early Intervention and Detection in Oncology**
- Included as a **dual-use** example: an at-home synthetic-biology biosensor platform (breath/urine) for multi-cancer early detection. The detection *technology* (over-the-counter synthetic biosensors) is directly transferable to infectious-disease point-of-care testing, though the programme target is cancer, **not** infection.
- **Years / scale:** Announced August 1, 2024; kickoff September 30, 2025. **Up to $147 million over 5 years**, plus ~$21 million corporate resource-sharing.

#### Rapid-response and distributed biomanufacturing

**GIVE — Genetic Medicines and Individualized Manufacturing for Everyone**
- **Purpose:** ARPA-H's flagship **distributed, on-demand biomanufacturing** programme — a multi-site network for RNA-based genetic medicines, with a vision to produce and dose "within a week of diagnosis." Directly relevant to rapid MCM (e.g., RNA vaccine/therapeutic) response.
- **Years / scale:** Solicitation launched ~September 25, 2025; **not yet awarded**. No programme-specific dollar figure published; a "nine-figure" characterization in FierceBiotech bundled GIVE with the (non-infectious) THRIVE programme and is **unverified for GIVE alone [FLAG]**.

**WHEAT — Wheat-germ cell-free API manufacturing**
- **Purpose:** Distributed/decentralized production of biologic and small-molecule **active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs)** using wheat-germ cell-free expression, to harden domestic pharmaceutical supply chains. The clearest funded rapid/distributed-manufacturing award.
- **Years / scale:** **Up to $29 million**, two-year programme; award start March 28, 2025. **[FLAG — PM not publicly named.]**

**SUPPLI — AI-guided pharmaceutical supply-chain shortage tool**
- **Purpose:** AI tool to calculate shortage risk, reverse-engineer molecules to derive starting ingredients, and predict U.S.-based manufacturing options (focused on domesticating regulatory starting material).
- **Years / scale:** **[FLAG — acronym expansion, dollar amount, year, and PM all unverified (award page not retrievable). Awardee is confirmed (see Section 2).]**

**BoSS — BioStabilization Systems**
- **Purpose:** Room-temperature production, storage, and shipping of cells/biologics to eliminate cold-chain dependence (distribution resilience for MCMs).
- **Years / scale:** Solicitation open (~December 2025); **not yet awarded**; no public dollar figure.

**AdVAntage — cell-free viral-vector manufacturing** (Health Science Futures)
- **Purpose:** Cell-free, self-assembling viral-vector manufacturing as an alternative to cell-based AAV production (a rapid/scalable manufacturing platform).
- **Years / scale:** **Up to $13.4 million** (start January 17, 2025).

#### Adjacent / partially relevant programmes (noted for completeness)

- **DIGIHEALS (Digital Health Security)** — protects digital health infrastructure from cyberattacks; **not infectious-disease detection** despite the "Digital Health Security" name. Announced August 2023; six awardees totaling **up to $50 million**.
- **PARADIGM** — rural distributed hospital-level care (includes miniaturized diagnostics like portable CT; no infectious-disease point-of-care emphasis).
- **RAPID** — AI/ML diagnostics for **rare disease**, not infection.

#### Names that are NOT ARPA-H infectious-disease programmes (corrections to avoid misattribution)

- **POSE** is an **NSF** open-source programme, not ARPA-H (likely confused with POSEIDON).
- **ProSeRa** does not exist at ARPA-H (likely a garble of **PROSPR**, an aging/healthspan programme).
- **NITRO** is real but addresses **osteoarthritis/joint regeneration**, not infection.
- "POSE," "NOW," or a standalone "rapid response" programme with infectious-disease awardees **do not exist** at ARPA-H.
- The HHS **"$100M broad-spectrum antiviral SMART Prize"** is a **BARDA/ASPR** effort, not ARPA-H.

### Negative findings (to prevent misattribution)

- No confirmed ARPA-H award to **National Resilience** was found.
- **VitaKey's** up-to-$62M award is under Proactive Health for **nutrition/obesity**, not biomanufacturing — a common conflation.

- Claimed ARPA-H diagnostic awards to **Sherlock Biosciences, Cue Health, or Cepheid** could **not be verified** — treat as unconfirmed.
- No confirmed ARPA-H award found specifically for **influenza, pan-coronavirus, or thermostable vaccines** (pan-coronavirus hits trace to NIAID/NIH, not ARPA-H).
