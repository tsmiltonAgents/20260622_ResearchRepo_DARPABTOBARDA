# U.S. Department of State (Biosecurity & Global Health Security)

## Introduction
State funds biosecurity/global-health-security work along two traditions: **arms control/nonproliferation** (Bureau of International Security and Nonproliferation, ISN) — incl. the **Biosecurity Engagement Program (BEP)** and U.S. coordination of the G7 **Global Partnership** — and **global health diplomacy** (the **Bureau of Global Health Security and Diplomacy, GHSD**, est. 2023, which houses PEPFAR). Two caveats: much BEP funding flows through intermediaries (esp. **CRDF Global**) with downstream subawards not itemized publicly; and multilateral contributions (Pandemic Fund, CEPI, Gavi) historically flowed via USAID/Treasury — State-specific attribution applies mainly from 2025–2026 after USAID folded into State. Figures flagged; nothing invented.

## Section 1 — Programmes

### 1.1 ISN / Office of Cooperative Threat Reduction (ISN/CTR)
Manages the **Global Threat Reduction (GTR)** program (funded from the NADR account). *Distinct from the DoD/DTRA Nunn-Lugar CTR.* GTR request: ~$91.1M (FY23), ~$96.6M (FY24) — covers all GTR programs (BEP, Chemical Security, etc.).

### 1.2 Biosecurity Engagement Program (BEP)
First funded FY2006. Mission: improve biosecurity, biosafety, pathogen/disease surveillance, and scientist engagement worldwide; secure "especially dangerous pathogens"; reduce dual-use misuse. Small grants (typically <$500K). Geography: South/Southeast Asia and Middle East originally, later Africa, former Soviet Union, Latin America. **No published standalone BEP budget** (folded into GTR); BEP solicitations cited $25M (ISN-ISNCTR-15-001) and ~$14M (2021) available. *(Distinct from the larger DoD/DTRA CBEP/BTRP.)*

### 1.3 Bureau of Global Health Security and Diplomacy (GHSD)
Launched Aug 1, 2023 (consolidating PEPFAR's office, Office of International Health and Biodefense, and the COVID-19 Response coordinator; ~350 HQ staff). Leads global health security coordination and **PEPFAR** (>$130B cumulative; ~26M lives saved). Supports the **Global Health Security Agenda (GHSA)** ("more than $2B since 2015"). 2025: **USAID dissolved into State (July 1, 2025)**, global-health functions merged into GHSD, which was itself restructured (May 29, 2025).

### 1.4 Biological Weapons nonproliferation (BWC support, ISN/NDF)
State (ISN) coordinates U.S. support for the **Biological Weapons Convention** (in force 1975; ~187 parties) and its Implementation Support Unit (est. 2006). Combined "NPT & BWC Cooperation" line: $9M (FY24)/$6M (FY25)/$5M (FY26). The **Nonproliferation and Disarmament Fund (ISN/NDF)** (est. 1994) is a rapid-response mechanism (e.g., 2005 destruction of 72 dual-use fermenters at Biokombinat, Almaty).

### 1.5 Global Partnership Against the Spread of WMD (GP)
G7-led, 31-member; U.S. (State) coordinates. Launched at the 2002 Kananaskis G8 Summit ("10 plus 10 over 10" — up to $20B over 10 yrs, U.S. pledging $10B). Has a **Biological Security Working Group** (current flagship: SIMBA — Signature Initiative to Mitigate Biological Threats in Africa). By 2016 the GP had allocated >$21B, ~half from the U.S.

### 1.6 Contributions to multilateral funds (attribution caveat)
Flowed historically via USAID/Treasury, not a single State line; State-specific mainly 2025–2026+; pledged ≠ disbursed. **Pandemic Fund** (World Bank, 2022): U.S. up to $667M through 2026 (Treasury-governed). **CEPI**: U.S. NOT a founding govt donor; ~$217M cumulative; one clearly State-attributed pledge — **$50M via GHSD (June 12, 2026)** for Bundibugyo virus countermeasures. **Gavi**: U.S. a founding donor; FY24–FY26 $300M/yr appropriated — but **2025 reversal**: U.S. withheld funding and did not pledge at the Gavi 6.0 summit (June 2025).

## Section 2 — Grantees / Awardees

### 2.1 CRDF Global — BEP's primary grant-management partner
Independent nonprofit administering biosecurity work for State/DoD/DOE; ~$300M total awards cumulatively across all programs (not BEP-specific). BEP-linked projects: biorisk trainings in Indonesia (with LSU NCBRT), Ukraine (with EMPHNET), Jordan EOC. *(Large CRDF/State figures that are NOT BEP: a $7M ISN/NDF CBW-terrorism award; an $8M FIRST nuclear award.)* No published "CRDF under BEP" total.

### 2.2 National Academies of Sciences
Confirmed BEP-funded: the 2011 Istanbul workshop on high-containment-lab biosecurity (contracts STAT-8740/STAT-6253). *(A related NAS project PGA-CISAC-16-04 is DoD-sponsored, not BEP.)*

### 2.3 Other BEP implementers / labs
**Sandia National Laboratories** (Biosafety Twinning, BEP-funded); **LSU NCBRT/ACE** (biological/chemical threat training); **Global Implementation Solutions** (One Health projects, with Gryphon Scientific, Human Link); **EMPHNET**; partner-country labs (Pakistan institutions; DRC INRB — attribution to BEP uncertain). *(CH2M Hill, Battelle, Bechtel associated with DoD/DTRA CBEP, not confirmed BEP.)*

### 2.4 GHSD / PEPFAR grantees
Coordinated mainly through USAID (~60%) and CDC (~37%). Largest multilateral partner: **Global Fund** (FY2024 $1.65B; cumulative ~$33B; FY2026 cut to $1.25B). Named implementer example: FHI 360 EpiC.

## Key uncertainties
No public standalone BEP line (in GTR); CRDF/BEP downstream subawards opaque; BEP not separable in USAspending; multilateral fund attribution to State accurate mainly 2025–26+; 2025–26 aid freezes/rescissions in flux; several state.gov figures snippet-sourced and flagged.
