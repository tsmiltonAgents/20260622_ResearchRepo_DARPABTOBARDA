# U.S. Intelligence Community — IARPA & In-Q-Tel (Bio Research & Strategic Investment)

## Introduction
The IC pursues biology/biosecurity via two mechanisms: **IARPA** (the IC's high-risk R&D arm) funds multi-year programs around detecting engineered DNA, screening synthetic nucleic acids, and DNA data storage; **In-Q-Tel (IQT)** (the not-for-profit strategic investor founded 1999) takes equity stakes in startups and runs a biosecurity unit, **B.Next**. The **National Intelligence Council (NIC)** produces strategic pandemic analysis. Caveats: **IARPA contract values often undisclosed**; **IQT investment amounts almost never disclosed** (commonly ~$500K–$3M range, but specifics rarely public). Awardees/portfolio are representative, not exhaustive.

---

## Section 1 — Programmes

### IARPA biology / biosecurity programs

**Fun GCAT (Functional Genomic and Computational Assessment of Threats)** — computational/bioinformatics tools to screen nucleic-acid sequences and functionally annotate "genes of concern," improving DNA-synthesis screening by *function* not just sequence. PM John Julias; BAA 2016 (IARPA-BAA-16-08), contracts by early 2018, three phases. One disclosed figure: **$1.9M** Phase 3 to the Signature Science team (Apr 2020).

**FELIX (Finding Engineering-Linked Indicators)** — computational + experimental tools to detect engineered biological systems (early warning of man-made biothreats). Program completed **Oct 17, 2022**. Deliverables: Ginkgo Bioworks' **ENDAR** computational tools; Draper's experimental detection platform. Contract values not disclosed.

**MIST (Molecular Information Storage)** — DNA-based deployable digital data storage at exabyte scale. IARPA committed ~**$48M**; two teams up to **$23M** (Molecular Encoding Consortium) and **$25M** (Georgia Tech). Awards Jan 2020.

**Adjacent:** the Open Source Indicators (OSI) program included early outbreak forecasting (broad event-forecasting, not a dedicated bio program).

### In-Q-Tel — biotech thesis & B.Next
IQT (founded 1999; Tysons VA; legally independent of CIA) invests across engineering biology, prioritizing the **biotech-AI convergence**. **B.Next** (est. 2015) is its biodefense unit — a "biodefense technology architecture" identifying needs in (1) digital surveillance/outbreak detection, (2) vaccine design/manufacturing/delivery, (3) synbio diagnostics/therapeutics. Led by Dr. Tara O'Toole (former DHS Under Secretary for S&T). Active early in COVID-19 (physical-distancing/contact-tracing analyses).

## Section 2 — Grantees / Awardees / Portfolio

### IARPA performers (representative)
- **Fun GCAT:** Signature Science (prime; with Rice, U Maryland, Fraunhofer USA) — S2FAST software, $1.9M Phase 3; Virginia Tech Biocomplexity Institute-led team; a team with Twist Bioscience, Battelle, Ginkgo Bioworks, One Codex; JHU APL (portal host).
- **FELIX:** Ginkgo Bioworks (ENDAR) and Draper (experimental platform).
- **MIST:** Georgia Tech Research Institute "SMASH" (with Twist Bioscience, Roswell Biotechnologies, U Washington/Microsoft, up to $25M); Molecular Encoding Consortium (Broad Institute/Robert Nicol, Harvard/Donhee Ham, DNA Script, up to $23M).

### In-Q-Tel bio-relevant portfolio (amounts undisclosed per IQT practice)
**Confirmed:** T2 Biosystems (magnetic-resonance pathogen diagnostics; part of ~$10.8M round), Claremont BioSolutions (nucleic-acid sample prep, 2012), Quanterix (Simoa ultra-sensitive detection, 2012), Kailos Genetics (NGS lab/software, 2017), OpGen (optical genome mapping, ~2008/2012), Bio-NEMS (handheld DNA testing, 2013), Antheia (synbio API biomanufacturing, $17M round, 2024), Alta Resource Technologies (engineered-protein mineral separation, 2025).
**Uncertain:** Ginkgo Bioworks — confirmed IARPA *performer* (FELIX/Fun GCAT) but a direct IQT *equity* stake is aggregator-only/unverified.
**Not confirmed (corrections):** Biomeme (no IQT investment found — backed by FIND; the "handheld DNA" IQT deal is Bio-NEMS, a different company).

### National Intelligence Council (pandemic intelligence)
- Jan 2000 NIE "The Global Infectious Disease Threat" — warned a "killer" flu pandemic was "not a question of whether, but when."
- *Global Trends* (2004+) repeatedly flagged pandemics as high-impact scenarios.
- COVID-19 origins assessment (declassified summary Aug 27, 2021): virus judged *not* a bioweapon and *probably not* engineered (low confidence); IC divided between natural-spillover and lab-incident hypotheses.

## Key uncertainties
IARPA values public only where performers disclosed (Fun GCAT Phase 3 $1.9M; MIST ~$48M / $23M+$25M); all IQT amounts undisclosed; Ginkgo IQT-equity unverified (IARPA role solid); Biomeme is a likely misattribution (Bio-NEMS is the real handheld-DNA IQT deal); IQT reports 800+ total investments so more bio companies likely exist.
