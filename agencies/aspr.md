# ASPR — Administration for Strategic Preparedness and Response (beyond BARDA)

## Introduction
ASPR is the HHS operating division leading medical/public-health preparedness and response. Elevated from a staff office to a full operating division in 2022. Its best-known component, **BARDA**, is documented separately (referenced here only for context). This file covers ASPR's **non-BARDA** functions: PHEMCE, the Strategic National Stockpile, Project BioShield stewardship, the Hospital Preparedness Program, the Regional Disaster Health Response System, and the industrial-base/supply-chain mission. ASPR's total FY2024 enacted budget was ~**$3.6B** (BARDA ~$1.02B). Several supply-chain/procurement awards are issued jointly "HHS/ASPR/BARDA" — flagged to avoid double-counting.

---

## Section 1 — Programmes / Mechanisms

### 1.1 PHEMCE (Public Health Emergency Medical Countermeasures Enterprise)
Federal interagency body coordinating MCMs against CBRN + EID threats. **Chaired/led by ASPR**; Governance Board includes CDC, FDA, NIH + DHS, DoD, VA, USDA. Created 2006, **reconstituted Feb 2022**. A policy/coordination "shop," not an appropriation; directs how MCM dollars (BioShield SRF, SNS, BARDA) are prioritized. Has a 2023–2027 Multiyear Budget and a 2024 Strategy & Implementation Plan.

### 1.2 Strategic National Stockpile (SNS)
Largest national supply of MCMs/supplies. Established 1998 (NPS); renamed 2003; **transferred CDC→ASPR Oct 1, 2018** (PAHPA). ~**$7B enterprise**; **FY2024 enacted $980M** (FY2023 ~$965M). Holdings: smallpox/anthrax vaccines, antibiotics, antitoxins, CHEMPACKs (1,340+ locations), ventilators, PPE. Drew GAO/OIG scrutiny for COVID readiness.

### 1.3 Project BioShield Special Reserve Fund (SRF) — stewardship
Created by the Project BioShield Act 2004; original **$5.6B** SRF. ASPR stewards; BARDA executes acquisitions for ~17 DHS material threats. PAHPRA 2013 set ~$2.8B for FY2014–18. Modern annual line: **$825M enacted FY2024**; FY2027 request ~$725M. *(Product acquisitions belong in BARDA docs; the appropriation/stewardship is ASPR-level.)*

### 1.4 Hospital Preparedness Program (HPP)
ASPR's primary cooperative agreement for health-care system readiness; funds **Health Care Coalitions**. Only federal source specifically for health-care-delivery preparedness. **62 recipients**. ~**$240–250M/year** (FY2024 $240M); +~$526M COVID supplemental in FY2020.

### 1.5 Regional Disaster Health Response System (RDHRS)
Tiered regional pilot linking trauma centers, hospitals, HCCs, EMS, public health. FY2018 pilots (~$3M/yr each): **Massachusetts General Hospital** (Region 1), **Nebraska Medicine** (Region 7); third site added 2020.

### 1.6 Industrial-Base / Supply-Chain (IBMSC) + DPA Title III
Born from COVID shortages; the **Office/Center for Industrial Base Management & Supply Chain (IBMSC)**, formerly Industrial Base Expansion (IBx). Works via **Defense Production Act Title III** to onshore APIs/KSMs, PPE, swabs, syringes. Publishes the National Strategy for a Resilient Public Health Supply Chain.

### 1.7 NIIMBL
The National Institute for Innovation in Manufacturing Biopharmaceuticals is principally a **NIST (Commerce)** cooperative-agreement recipient; ASPR/BARDA engagement is collaborative (pandemic biomanufacturing, the "130-day vaccine" goal). **Not counted as a major ASPR award.**

### 1.8 RRPV / Project NextGen (context only — BARDA-level)
Mentioned for org context; their awards are BARDA awards (documented in `allgrants.md`).

## Section 2 — Grantees / Awardees (non-BARDA)

### 2.1 HPP recipients (complete set of 62)
50 states + DC + NYC, LA County, Chicago + Puerto Rico + Northern Mariana Islands + American Samoa, Guam, USVI + FSM, Palau, Marshall Islands. Each passes funds to in-jurisdiction Health Care Coalitions. ~$240–250M/yr base + ~$526M COVID supplemental (FY2020).

### 2.2 RDHRS pilots
Massachusetts General Hospital (~$3M/yr, FY2018+); Nebraska Medicine (~$3M/yr, FY2018+); third site (2020).

### 2.3 SNS procurement vendors (representative)
Emergent BioSolutions (anthrax vaccine, cumulatively ~$1B — *often BioShield/BARDA-executed, flagged*); DuPont (~2.25M Tyvek suits, 2020); Sandoz/Bayer (HCQ/chloroquine donations 2020); ventilator makers (GE, Hill-Rom, Medtronic, ResMed, Vyaire, Hamilton, Zoll). *2020 COVID procurement amounts/executing-office inconsistently disclosed.*

### 2.4 DPA Title III awardees (representative)
Manus Bio ($32.4M, Nov 25 2024, KSMs); Antheia (up to $12M, Nov 25 2024 + earlier ~$12M, API fermentation). The two Nov 2024 awards totaled $44.4M. **Phlow Corporation $354M** (2020, essential medicines/APIs with Civica Rx, VCU M4ALL, AMPAC) was announced "HHS/ASPR/BARDA" and is typically a **BARDA** award — do not double-count.

### 2.5 NIIMBL
Principally a NIST grantee, not ASPR — included for completeness only.

## Key caveats
HPP 62-recipient list is complete/fixed; SNS vendors and DPA Title III are representative; Project BioShield SRF procurements and Phlow $354M run through BARDA (flagged); 2020 COVID SNS amounts/offices inconsistently disclosed; budget figures are FY2024 enacted unless noted; "up to $X" are ceilings.
