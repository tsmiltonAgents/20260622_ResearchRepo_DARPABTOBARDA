# DARPA BTO and BARDA Programmes — Complete Catalogue

> Research compiled 2026-06-22. All entries are source-backed (URLs inline). Two agencies are covered:
> - **DARPA BTO** — the Biological Technologies Office of the U.S. Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DoD), established April 2014.
> - **BARDA** — the Biomedical Advanced Research and Development Authority, part of the HHS Administration for Strategic Preparedness and Response (ASPR), established December 2006.
>
> A "Pandemic / infectious-disease relevance" flag is applied to every entry: **YES** = directly about infectious disease / pandemic / biothreat; **PARTIAL** = biodefense-adjacent or dual-use; **NO** = unrelated. This flag drives the Phase 2 grants/contracts research (`allgrants.md`).

# PART 1 — DARPA Biological Technologies Office (BTO)

**Office context.** DARPA established BTO in **April 2014**, consolidating biology-focused programs that previously lived in the Defense Sciences Office (DSO) and Microsystems Technology Office (MTO). BTO's remit spans infectious disease/biosecurity, synthetic biology and biomanufacturing, neurotechnology and human-machine interfaces, human performance, and environmental/agricultural biology.
Sources: [darpa.mil/about/offices/bto](https://www.darpa.mil/about/offices/bto) · [Wikipedia: BTO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biological_Technologies_Office_(DARPA))

**Data caveats.** DARPA program pages are largely archival and often omit PM names and exact dates; PM names/date ranges below are drawn from BAAs, press releases, and PM bios, and some PMs rotated over a program's life. "Complete" programs rarely publish an end year; year ranges are estimates from BAA/launch dates plus DARPA's typical ~4-year program duration. Several programs commonly grouped with BTO actually sit in other offices (DSO, I2O, TTO) — these are flagged in §1.7.

## 1.2 Biosecurity / Health / Pandemic / Infectious Disease

### ADEPT — Autonomous Diagnostics to Enable Prevention and Therapeutics
Rapidly identify and respond to natural/engineered disease and toxin threats via self-administered diagnostics, multiplexed point-of-care tests, and nucleic-acid (DNA/RNA) approaches to immunity. Direct predecessor to P3 (DARPA groups them as "ADEPT/P3"). ~2011–2016, complete. PM: Dr. Daniel Wattendorf. **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/autonomous-diagnostics-to-enable-prevention-and-therapeutics)

### P3 — Pandemic Prevention Platform
End-to-end platform to discover, produce, and deliver DNA/RNA-encoded medical countermeasures (e.g., antibody therapeutics) against any infectious threat within 60 days of identification. Early antibody work fed the 2020 COVID-19 response. ~2017–2021, complete. PMs: Col. Matthew Hepburn (early); Dr. Amy Jenkins (COVID phase). **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/pandemic-prevention-platform)

### PREEMPT — PReventing EMerging Pathogenic Threats
Get ahead of zoonotic spillover by targeting/suppressing viral pathogens in animal reservoirs and insect vectors before they jump to humans, using multiscale predictive models and scalable prevention technologies. ~2018–2022, complete. PM: Dr. Jim Gimlett. **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/preventing-emerging-pathogenic-threats)

### Prophecy (Pathogen Defeat)
Shift drug/vaccine development from reactive to predictive by forecasting the rate, direction, and phenotype of viral mutations, enabling countermeasures before new strains emerge. ~2010–early 2010s, complete. PM: Dr. Michael Callahan (launch). **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/prophecy-pathogen-defeat)

### INTERCEPT — INTERfering and Co-Evolving Prevention and Therapy
Harness viral evolution via therapeutic interfering particles (TIPs) — defective virus-derived particles that replicate only alongside the target virus, outcompete it, and co-evolve to resist viral escape. Targets included Ebola, SARS, Dengue, Zika, Chikungunya. ~2016–2020, complete. PM: Dr. Jim Gimlett. **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/intercept)

### THoR — Technologies for Host Resilience
Optimize force health against emerging infectious diseases by focusing on host *tolerance* (maintaining fitness during active infection) rather than attacking the pathogen. ~2015–2019, complete. PM: Col. Matt Hepburn. **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/technologies-for-host-resilience)

### PREPARE — PReemptive Expression of Protective Alleles and Response Elements
Programmable, reversible gene-modulating medical countermeasures (PGM-MCMs) that temporarily activate the body's own protective genes without altering the genetic code. Demonstrated against influenza, organophosphate poisoning, gamma irradiation; pivoted to mRNA/Cas13a SARS-CoV-2 therapeutics during COVID. ~2018–2022, complete. PM: Renee Wegrzyn. **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/preemptive-expression-of-protective-alleles-and-response-elements)

### DIGET — Detect It with Gene Editing Technologies
CRISPR-based field-forward diagnostics/biosurveillance: a disposable point-of-need device (up to ~10 pathogens + host biomarkers) and a massively multiplexed device (1,000+ targets). 2019–~2024, complete. PM: Renee Wegrzyn. (Prime: MRIGlobal; subs incl. Mammoth Biosciences, Draper.) **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/detect-it-with-gene-editing-technologies)

### NOW — Nucleic acids On-demand Worldwide
Mobile, CONEX-box-sized platform to produce/formulate/package hundreds of doses of DNA/RNA vaccines and therapeutics in under 24 hours for field response. Announced Feb 2021; ~5-year awards (GE Research ~$41M; DNA Script + Moderna). PM: Dr. Amy Jenkins. **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/nucleic-acids-on-demand-worldwide)

### ECHO — Epigenetic CHaracterization and Observation
Portable point-of-care device reading an individual's epigenetic "fingerprint" from a small sample in ≤30 minutes to reveal history of exposure to threat agents; also positioned for rapid infection diagnostics. ~2018–2023, complete. PM: Dr. Eric Van Gieson. **Pandemic: PARTIAL** (primarily WMD/exposure detection). [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/epigenetic-characterization-and-observation)

### Friend or Foe
Portable high-throughput platform to rapidly screen unfamiliar bacteria from environmental samples to establish pathogenicity, including against engineered organisms designed to evade detection. Announced Feb 2018; ~$14.2M Texas A&M-led. PM: Dr. Paul Sheehan. **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/friend-or-foe)

### RTA — Rapid Threat Assessment
Within 30 days of exposure, map the complete molecular mechanism by which a chem/bio threat agent alters human cellular processes, to accelerate countermeasures. ~2013–2018, complete. PM: Barry Pallotta. **Pandemic: PARTIAL.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/rapid-threat-assessment)

### HEALR — Harnessing Enzymatic Activity for Lifesaving Remedies
Host-driven therapeutics recruiting the body's own machinery (PROTAC-style targeted protein degradation / "NewTACs") to degrade pathogen targets — aimed at multi-drug-resistant and emerging bacterial/microbial infections. Announced 2020; teams selected 2021; active. PMs: Seth M. Cohen (launch); Jeremy Pamplin (current). **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/healr)

### AIM — Assessing Immune Memory
Systems-level tool to predict early whether a vaccine candidate will provide durable, long-lasting human immune protection. Absorbed the "Outpacing Infectious Disease" effort. Teams selected 2023. **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/assessing-immune-memory)

### Panacea
Rapidly discover/design network-level multi-target drugs for physiological resilience. During COVID produced the first human–SARS-CoV-2 protein interactome and identified candidates (zotatifin, plitidepsin). ~2018–2022, legacy. **Pandemic: PARTIAL.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/news/2021/covid-19)

### PPB — Personalized Protective Biosystem
Lightweight materials plus tissue-protective countermeasures at skin, eyes, and airway for on-demand broad-spectrum chem-bio protection. ~2019–2023, complete. PM: Eric Van Gieson. **Pandemic: PARTIAL.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/personalized-protective-biosystem)

### Biostasis
Controllably slow biochemical reactions in living systems ("slow life to save life") to extend the trauma "golden hour." 2018–~2023, complete/transitioning. PM: Tristan McClure-Begley. **Pandemic: PARTIAL.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/news/2018/slowing-biological-time)

### DLT — Dialysis-Like Therapeutics
Portable device that removes "dirty" blood, separates harmful agents (e.g., in sepsis), and returns "clean" blood. ~2012–2016, complete. **Pandemic: PARTIAL** (sepsis/bloodstream infection). [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biological_Technologies_Office_(DARPA))

### Battlefield Medicine — Pharmacy on Demand (PoD) / Bio-MOD
Miniaturized platforms for on-site production of small-molecule APIs (PoD) and therapeutic proteins/biologics (Bio-MOD, target <24 h). ~2012–2016, complete. PMs: Geoffrey Ling; Tyler McQuade. **Pandemic: PARTIAL** (rapid MCM manufacturing). [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/battlefield-medicine)

### Focused Pharma
Rapidly characterize natural-product compounds to develop novel neuropsychiatric drugs. ~2018–2022, complete. **Pandemic: NO.** [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biological_Technologies_Office_(DARPA))

### MPS — Microphysiological Systems
"Organ-on-a-chip" human-tissue platforms reconstituting organ structure/function for drug, vaccine, and toxin testing. ~2011–2017, complete. **Pandemic: PARTIAL.** [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biological_Technologies_Office_(DARPA))

### IVN — In Vivo Nanoplatforms
Adaptable nanoparticles for persistent, distributed physiologic and environmental sensing, plus treatment. ~2012–2017, complete. **Pandemic: PARTIAL.** [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biological_Technologies_Office_(DARPA))

### Prometheus
Host-resilience / contagion-prediction effort (no expansion published); on the canonical BTO list. ~2010s. **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biological_Technologies_Office_(DARPA))

### Bio-Attribution Challenge
Prize competition to analyze petabyte-scale datasets in near real-time to trace biological-sequence origin and identify signatures of engineered pathogens (bio-forensics). Data curated by LLNL. Launched late 2025. PM: Abhishek Singharoy. **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/challenges/bio-attribution)

## 1.3 Synthetic Biology / Engineering Biology / Biomanufacturing

### Living Foundries (umbrella: ATCG + 1000 Molecules)
Turn synthetic biology into a predictable engineering discipline: compress the design-build-test-learn cycle ≥10x (**ATCG**) and produce high-value molecules on demand (**1000 Molecules** — performers produced 1,600+ molecules/precursors). 2010 (pre-BTO, in MTO) – transitioned to five service labs Dec 2021; complete. PMs: Alicia Jackson, Justin Gallivan (early); Renée Wegrzyn, Anne Cheever (later). **Pandemic: NO.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/living-foundries)

### BRICS — Biological Robustness In Complex Settings
Increase robustness, stability, and safety/containment of engineered microbial biosystems for reliable DoD use. 2014–2018, complete. PM: Dr. Justin Gallivan. **Pandemic: PARTIAL** (biosafety/biocontainment). [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/biological-robustness-in-complex-settings)

### ELM — Engineered Living Materials
Living biomaterials combining structural properties of building materials with attributes of living systems (rapid in-situ growth, self-repair). 2016–~2020, complete. PM: Justin Gallivan. **Pandemic: NO.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/engineered-living-materials)

### Safe Genes
"Safety by design" for genome editing: genetic control of editors, molecular countermeasures, and genetic remediation (including countering gene drives). 2016–2021, complete. PM: Renée Wegrzyn. **Pandemic: PARTIAL/YES** (dual-use; vector-control work tied to dengue/Zika). [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/safe-genes)

### B-SAFE — Broad-Spectrum Antagonists For Editors
Platform technologies for potent inhibitors across multiple classes/species of gene editors, via computational/deep-learning discovery. 2024–present, active. PM: Shannon Greene. **Pandemic: PARTIAL.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/news/2024/inhibiting-gene-editors)

### Biological Control
New capabilities for control of biological systems across scales via embedded biological controllers; cites combating biological threats and host defenses against emerging diseases. ~2016–2020, complete. **Pandemic: PARTIAL.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/program/biological-control)

### B-SURE — Biomanufacturing: Survival, Utility, and Reliability beyond Earth
Fundamental science on how industrial biomanufacturing microbes perform in space. 2022–present, active. PM: Anne Cheever. **Pandemic: NO.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/biomanufacturing-survival-utility-and-reliability-beyond-earth)

### EMBER — Environmental Microbes as a BioEngineering Resource
Bioengineered microbes to separate and purify rare earth elements as a domestic alternative to mining. 2022–present, active. PM: Linda Chrisey. **Pandemic: NO.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/program/environmental-microbes-as-a-bioengineering-resource)

### ReSource
Self-contained systems that break down mixed military waste and reformulate it into fuels, lubricants, adhesives, water, and edible macronutrients. ~2019–2025. PM: Blake Bextine. **Pandemic: NO.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/news/2021/resource-program)

### Cornucopia
Deployable on-demand production of nutritionally complete food from microbes using air, water, and electricity. Announced Dec 2021; ~4-year. PMs: Molly Jahn; William Mounfield. **Pandemic: NO.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/cornucopia)

### BLUE — BioLogical Undersea Energy
Power supplies converting marine biomass into electrical power for ocean-deployed sensors. Announced Feb 2024. PM: Leonard Tender. **Pandemic: NO.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/biological-undersea-energy)

### Switch
Agile, reprogrammable biomanufacturing for supply-chain resilience. Announced July 2024. PM: Matthew Pava. **Pandemic: NO.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/news/2024/programmable-biomanufacturing)

### Fleetwood
Produce critical chemicals from domestic resources via biomass-waste conversion and lignin upcycling. ~2024–present. **Pandemic: NO.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/about/offices/bto)

### Smart-RBC — Smart Red Blood Cells
Engineer enucleated red blood cells to temporarily enhance warfighter physiological resilience. ~2025–present. PM: Lt. Cmdr. Robert Murray. **Pandemic: NO.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/smart-rbc)

### GO — Generative Optogenetics
Light-directed, template-free DNA/RNA synthesis inside living cells. 2025–present. **Pandemic: NO.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/work-with-us/opportunities/darpa-ps-26-10)

### O-Circuit — Organoid Cytomorphic Intelligence...
Engineered organoid-based "Biological Processing Units" for low-power edge AI. Solicitation ~2026. **Pandemic: NO.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/o-circuit)

### RadioBio
Determine whether purposeful electromagnetic signaling between biological cells exists. Feb 2017–~2020, complete. PM: Michael Fiddy. **Pandemic: NO.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/news/2017/radiobio)

## 1.5 Environmental / Agricultural / Ecological / Bio-Sensing

### APT — Advanced Plant Technologies
Engineer plants as self-sustaining ground-based sensors that detect/report CBRNE threats. 2017–~2022, complete. PM: Blake Bextine. **Pandemic: PARTIAL** (biological-threat detection). [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/advanced-plant-technologies)

### Insect Allies (a.k.a. HEGAA — Horizontal Environmental Genetic Alteration Agents)
Use insects to deliver gene-modified plant viruses conferring protective traits to mature crops within one growing season. Drew dual-use/bioweapon controversy; all work in closed facilities. 2016–2021, complete. PM: Blake Bextine. **Pandemic: PARTIAL** (agricultural biosecurity). [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/insect-allies)

### ReVector
Temporarily and safely modify the human skin microbiome to alter volatile chemicals, reducing mosquito attraction (~2 weeks) to cut transmission of malaria, dengue, chikungunya. 2019–~2023, complete. PM: Dr. Linda Chrisey (earlier Eric Van Gieson). **Pandemic: YES** (vector-borne disease prevention). [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/revector)

### PALS — Persistent Aquatic Living Sensors
Tap natural sensing abilities of marine organisms to detect underwater vehicles. 2018–2021, complete. PM: Dr. Lori Adornato. **Pandemic: NO.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/persistent-aquatic-living-sensors)

### BioReporters for Subterranean Surveillance
Use plants to sense subsurface CBRNE threats and propagate a surface signal. 2019–~2021, complete (performers: Raytheon BBN, Signature Science). **Pandemic: PARTIAL.** [Source](https://nta.org/2019/11/20/darpa-awards-two-1-6m-contracts-for-biological-sensors-for-subterranean-surveillance/)

### Reefense
Self-healing hybrid biological/engineered reef-mimicking structures to mitigate coastal flooding/erosion. Performers announced June 2022; into ~2026. PM: Dr. Catherine Campbell. **Pandemic: PARTIAL** (coral/oyster disease resilience). [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/reefense)

### Tellus
Rapid design-build-test of microbe-based "sense-and-respond" devices for environmental monitoring. ~2023–present (performer: Draper). PM: Dr. Linda Chrisey. **Pandemic: NO/PARTIAL.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/tellus)

### GUARDIAN — Genetic Utilization for Advanced Regulation and Defense of Indigenous and Native Species
Safe, rapid genetic/biological suppression of invasive species (initial targets: New World Screwworm, Brown Tree Snake). ~2026, launching. PM: Catherine Campbell. **Pandemic: PARTIAL.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/guardian-genetic-utilization)

### Ag x BTO (Agricultural Defense)
Special topic area defending agriculture/food supply against natural and manmade threats (incl. chem/bio surveillance). Submissions opened June 2025; awards up to $500K. PM: Michael Koeris. **Pandemic: PARTIAL/YES.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/ag-bto)

### AMPHORA — Assured Microbial Preservation in Harsh Or Remote Areas
Cold-chain-free preservation of any microbe sample to support biosurveillance. Recent. **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs)

# PART 2 — Biomedical Advanced Research and Development Authority (BARDA)

**Agency context.** BARDA is a center within HHS/ASPR, established December 2006 under the Pandemic and All-Hazards Preparedness Act (PAHPA). It develops and procures medical countermeasures (MCMs) — vaccines, therapeutics, diagnostics, devices — against CBRN threats, pandemic influenza, and emerging infectious diseases, mainly via public-private partnerships and the Project BioShield Special Reserve Fund.
Sources: [Wikipedia: BARDA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biomedical_Advanced_Research_and_Development_Authority) · [medicalcountermeasures.gov/barda](https://medicalcountermeasures.gov/barda)

> Note: a "BEACON" program (in the original prompt) was **not found** in any authoritative source. The genuine DRIVe programs are ENACT, Solving Sepsis, ReDIRECT, and the BARDA Accelerator Network.

## 2.2 Core threat divisions

### CBRN Division (Chemical, Biological, Radiological & Nuclear)
Supports MCM development from preclinical work through FDA approval and procurement, funded largely via Project BioShield. ~125 CBRN products with 42 FDA approvals/licensures/clearances as of source data; delivered the first therapeutics against a filovirus and the first licensed Zaire ebolavirus vaccine. ~2007, active. **Pandemic: PARTIAL.** [Source](https://medicalcountermeasures.gov/barda/cbrn)
Sub-programs: **Antimicrobials** (PARTIAL); **Antivirals & Antitoxins** — botulism, smallpox, viral hemorrhagic fevers (PARTIAL); **Vaccines** — smallpox, anthrax, VHF, MDR threats (PARTIAL); **Chemical MCMs** (NO); **Radiological/Nuclear MCMs** (NO); **Burn and Blast MCMs** (NO).

### Influenza & Emerging Infectious Diseases (IEID / IEIDD) Division
Early detection and countermeasure development for pandemic influenza and emerging diseases; modernizing/expanding domestic manufacturing and improving licensed seasonal/pre-pandemic vaccines. ~2007, active. **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://medicalcountermeasures.gov/barda/influenza-and-emerging-infectious-diseases)
Sub-programs: **Pandemic Vaccines & Adjuvants** (manages NPIVS); **Vaccine Development** (goal: vaccine within 100 days, scale-up within 120 days); **Therapeutics** (antivirals, immune modulators for ARDS, long-acting antivirals for PrEP); **Influenza Diagnostics & Devices** (rapid at-home/pharmacy tests). All YES.

### National Pre-pandemic Influenza Vaccine Stockpile (NPIVS)
Maintains pre-pandemic bulk antigens/adjuvants from influenza viruses with pandemic potential (H2Nx, H5Nx, H7Nx, H9Nx, H1N1v, H3N2v) plus contracts with domestic manufacturers. Active. **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://aspr.hhs.gov/AboutASPR/ProgramOffices/BARDA/Pages/PI.aspx)

## 2.3 Cross-cutting divisions & support services
- **DDDI — Detection, Diagnostics & Devices Infrastructure**: testing/medical-device MCMs across all mission threats. PARTIAL. [Source](https://medicalcountermeasures.gov/barda/dddi)
- **DCD / DNCD / RQA / CMA**: enabling/services divisions (clinical development & Clinical Studies Network; nonclinical models; regulatory/quality; contracts — managing ~$1.7B annual spend across a >$60B portfolio). PARTIAL. [Source](https://medicalcountermeasures.gov/barda)
- **PCI — Pharmaceutical Countermeasures & Infrastructure**: manufacturing, distribution, administration, production scaling. PARTIAL. [Source](https://medicalcountermeasures.gov/barda)

## 2.4 DRIVe (Division of Research, Innovation, and Ventures)
Launched June 2018 as a business accelerator funding transformative health-security technologies (non-dilutive and dilutive funding, forward-deployed teams). Active. PARTIAL. [Source](https://drive.hhs.gov/about.html)
- **ENACT (Early Notification to Act, Control and Treat)**: technologies detecting infection before symptoms appear. YES. [Source](https://drive.hhs.gov/enact.html)
- **Solving Sepsis**: diagnostics, devices, post-sepsis monitoring across the sepsis continuum. PARTIAL. [Source](https://drive.hhs.gov/)
- **ReDIRECT**: repurpose therapeutics against chemical-exposure conditions. NO. [Source](https://drive.hhs.gov/redirect.html)
- **BARDA Accelerator Network (BAN)**: nationwide accelerator hubs (I-CREATE, Paratus, VANGUARD, SPARK, VITAL). YES. [Source](https://drive.hhs.gov/accelerators.html)

## 2.5 BARDA Ventures
Government equity-investment initiative partnering with the Global Health Investment Corporation (GHIC); committed a minimum $50M over five years, up to $500M over 10 years, across seven mission areas. Announced 2022, active. **Pandemic: YES.** [Source](https://drive.hhs.gov/ventures.html)

## 2.6 Funding mechanism: Project BioShield
Special Reserve Fund established 2004 (~$5.6B over ten years originally) for advanced development and procurement of CBRN countermeasures. Used for late-stage development, Phase IV trials, and procurement into the SNS. Active. **Pandemic: PARTIAL.** [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biomedical_Advanced_Research_and_Development_Authority)

## 2.7 Manufacturing & infrastructure programs
- **CIADM — Centers for Innovation in Advanced Development and Manufacturing**: est. June 2012 (post-H1N1) as public-private partnerships for domestic surge manufacturing. Three centers: Emergent BioSolutions (Baltimore/Bayview), Texas A&M University System (with FUJIFILM Diosynth), and a third (Novartis → Holly Springs). Contracts renewable up to 25 years; major COVID-19 vaccine manufacturing role. PARTIAL. [Source](https://bioprocessintl.com/business/regulatory-affairs/elucidation-centers-for-the-innovation-in-advanced-development-and-manufacturing/)
- **FFMN — Fill Finish Manufacturing Network**: surge fill-finish capacity during emergencies. ~2019, active. YES. [Source](https://medicalcountermeasures.gov/barda/core-services)
- **BioMaP-Consortium**: industry partners across the manufacturing supply chain. ~2023, active. YES. [Source](https://medicalcountermeasures.gov/barda/preparedness-partnerships)
- **SNS — Strategic National Stockpile (relationship)**: BARDA develops/procures, SNS stores/deploys. PARTIAL. [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biomedical_Advanced_Research_and_Development_Authority)

## 2.8 COVID-19 programs
- **Operation Warp Speed (OWS) — BARDA's role**: announced May 15, 2020; BARDA modified contracts and funded developers (Moderna ~$483M, AstraZeneca ~$1.2B; also Regeneron, Janssen, Sanofi Pasteur, Novavax); EUAs for 10 therapeutics. 2020, concluded. YES. [Source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Warp_Speed)
- **Antiviral Program for Pandemics (APP)**: announced June 17, 2021; ~$3B from the American Rescue Plan for next-gen COVID antivirals + discovery platforms (incl. up to $1.2B for AViDD Centers). Active. YES. [Source](https://www.niaid.nih.gov/news-events/biden-administration-invest-3-billion-american-rescue-plan-part-covid-19-antiviral)
- **Project NextGen**: ~$5B initiative announced April 2023 (with NIAID) for next-gen COVID vaccines/therapeutics across three lines of effort. Active. YES. [Source](https://medicalcountermeasures.gov/nextgen)

## 2.9 Newer partnership vehicles (2021–2026)
- **RRPV — Rapid Response Partnership Vehicle**: Other Transaction Authority consortium (managed by Advanced Technology International, awarded Oct 2023; reported ceiling up to ~$10B) across vaccines/therapeutics and MedTech. Active. YES. [Source](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/advanced-technology-international-to-manage-bardas-rapid-response-partnership-vehicle-ota-301947055.html)
- **Accelerated Preparedness Partnerships**: umbrella for newer consortium-based models (RRPV, BioMaP-Consortium, related vehicles). ~2023, active. YES. [Source](https://medicalcountermeasures.gov/barda/preparedness-partnerships)
